At the hearing the court will decide whether to appoint a conservator, who the 
conservator will be, and what authority the conservator will have. The respondent has 
the following rights: 

the right to attend the hearing; 

the right to choose his or her own attorney; 

the right to nominate a conservator; 

the right to limit the conservator’s authority to that needed for protection; 

the right to receive written reasons for appointing a conservator; 

the right to request that the hearing be open or closed to the public; 

the right to present evidence; 

the right to ask questions of witnesses; 

the right to be examined by a court-appointed physician; 

the right to be interviewed by a court-appointed visitor and to ask that the visitor 
interview the proposed conservator;  

The respondent and any person interested in the respondent have the right to ask the 
court for a hearing to end the conservatorship, to appoint a different conservator, or to 
change the conservator’s authority. 

If a conservator is appointed, the conservator will make decisions about the 
respondent’s property.
