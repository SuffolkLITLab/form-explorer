Notice Required 

•  Rule 42.11  of the Rules of Juvenile Protection Procedure requires that, if the parent or 

legal custodian complies with the conditions of the court order and the child  is returned 
home, including  under protective supervision or trial  home visit, the county attorney 
shall immediately file with the court and serve upon all  parties a notice stating the date 
the child was returned home. 

•  Notice should also be provided if the child is moved from one foster care location  to 

another foster care location. 

•  Notice of the child’s return home is intended to ensure that the judge and parties are 

aware of the parent’s progress and compliance with court orders. 

•  Notice of the child’s return home or to another foster care location is also intended to 

ensure that client contact by attorneys and guardians ad litem may continue. 

•  Notice of the child’s return home or to another foster care location is also intended to 
inform the court administrator  of the child’s location  so that written notice of future 
hearings may be served upon the child  and foster parent as required under Rule 44.04.
