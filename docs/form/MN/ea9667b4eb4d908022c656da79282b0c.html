What Is an Order for Protection/Kev Yuam Tiv Thaiv Yog Dab Tsi? 

An Order for Protection (OFP) is an order signed by a judicial officer (judge or referee) that may 
help protect you from domestic abuse. An OFP orders the abuser not to contact, harm, or 
threaten to harm you, your children, or other people the judicial officer agrees to list on the OFP. 
You can also ask the judicial officer to order the abuser to do certain things, or stop doing certain 
things, to help keep you safe. 
Kev Yuam Tiv Thaiv (OFP) yog ib txoj kev yuam uas kos npe los ntawm tus kws phua txim (kws 
phua txim los sis kws txiav txim) uas pab tiv thaiv tau koj ntawm txoj kev sib ceg sib ntaus hauv 
tsev. Tsab OFP muaj cov lus yuam kom tus neeg tsim txom tsis txhob muaj kev sib cuag, kev ua 
mob, los sis kev hem ua mob rau koj, koj cov me nyuam, los sis lwm cov tib neeg uas tus kws 
phua txim pom zoo teev rau hauv tsab OFP. Koj puav leej thov tau tus kws phua txim kom yuam 
tus neeg tsim txom ua qee yam, los sis tsis txhob ua qee yam, kom pab koj nyob nyab xeeb.

personally delivered to (served on) the respondent. There is no cost to the petitioner for serving the 
respondent and no cost for the respondent to request a hearing.
