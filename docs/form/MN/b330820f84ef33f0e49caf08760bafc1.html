INSTRUCTIONS 
Defendant’s Assignment of Bail to a Third Party 

Minn. Stat. § 629.53 

According to Minn. Stat. § 629.53 (https://www.revisor.mn.gov/statutes/cite/629.53), any bail deposited in 
a criminal case belongs to the defendant, even if someone else posted the bail on the defendant’s behalf.  
The defendant can ask the court, in writing, to return any refunded bail money to someone else.  The 
defendant may use the form Assignment of Bail to a Third Party (CRM602) to let the court know who 
should receive any refunded bail in the case. 

Important Notices and Resources 

The Court has forms and instructions, for some types of cases, as a general guide to the court process. 
These instructions explain the steps in more detail and answer common questions, but are not a full 
guide to the law. Court employees may be able to give general information on court rules and 
procedures, but they cannot give legal advice.

The Assignment of Bail to a Third Party form (CRM602) asks for the following information: 

•  The name and address of the person, or the business or organization, that the court should 

send any refunded bail to;  

•  The court file number (also known as case number); 
•  The defendant’s address, phone number, and email address (if they have one); and 
•  The defendant’s signature.

Signature Block 

 

 

The defendant must personally sign the Assignment of Bail to a Third Party form.  Print defendant’s 
name, address, phone number, and e-mail address in the blanks under the signature line.  Also fill in 
the date that defendant signed the form.  Note: This form cannot be signed by anyone other than 
the defendant.
