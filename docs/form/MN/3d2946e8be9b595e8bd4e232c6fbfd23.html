Innocent Owner Notice to Prosecuting Authority 

Minn. Stat. § 169A.63, subd. 7a (Alcohol) 
Minn. Stat. § 609.5314, subd. 1a (Controlled Substance) 

Any person, other than the driver alleged to have committed a designated offense, may assert an ownership 
interest in a vehicle seized under Minn. Stat. § 169A.63 (alcohol related vehicle seizure), or Minn. Stat.  
§ 609.5314 (controlled substances related vehicle seizure) by notifying the prosecuting authority in writing no 
later than 60 days after being served with the Notice of Seizure and Intent to Forfeit.   

The prosecuting authority is the attorney responsible for prosecuting the offense that resulted in the seizure 
(for example: Rice County Attorney’s Office).  Note: it is important to list the correct prosecuting authority. If you 
are not sure which agency you should list here, get legal advice. 

You can use this form to notify the prosecuting authority of an innocent owner claim. Note: Court staff cannot 
give legal advice.  If you are not sure how to use this form, get legal advice.   

Do not file the Innocent Owner Notice to Prosecuting Authority with the court. You may want to keep a copy of 
the form for your records. 

After receiving your Innocent Owner Notice, the prosecuting authority may release the vehicle to the person 
asserting an innocent owner claim, or may proceed with the forfeiture. If you have any questions, contact the 
prosecuting authority or get legal advice.
