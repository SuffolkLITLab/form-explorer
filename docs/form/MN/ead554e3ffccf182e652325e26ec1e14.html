Please read this Notice when you are asking the court for an Order for Protection (OFP).

Notice to Petitioner about Your            Information 

Ntawv Fab Seeb rau tus Neeg Foob txog Koj           Cov Ntaub Ntawv 

 

  Whatever information you give to the court will become public information as soon as 
the court receives notice that the Respondent has been served with the OFP or Order 
for Hearing.  

  Txhua yam ncauj lus koj muab cev rau lub tsev hais plaub yuav yog ncauj lus qhib rau 
laj mej pej xeem paub txog thaum twg lub tsev hais plaub txais kiag tau lus faj seeb 
tias tus Neeg Raug Foob tau txais qhov OFP los sis daim Ntawv Yuam Muaj Rooj Sib 
Hais lawm.  

•  You may, however, ask that your address and/or telephone number not be given to 

the Respondent and not made a part of the public file.  
Koj thov tau kom tsis txhob muab koj qhov chaw nyob thiab/los sis tus xov tooj qhia 
rau tus Neeg Raug Foob thiab tsis txhob muab ua ib feem ntawm koj cov ntaub 
ntawv uas laj mej pej xeem paub txog.  

•  How? By filing Confidential Address/Phone Request form (OFP107), available online 

at http://mncourts.gov/GetForms.aspx?c=17&f=318.  
Yuav ua li cas? Los ntawm txoj kev ua Tsab Ntawv Thov Ceev Zoo Cia Chaw 
Nyob/Xov Tooj  (OFP107), uas muaj nyob saum huab cua ntawm 
http://mncourts.gov/GetForms.aspx?c=17&f=318.  

 

  The court administrator’s office will deliver a copy of the Petition, any attachments, and the 
OFP or Order for Hearing to the sheriff, who will serve these documents on the Respondent. 
Lub chaw khiav tsev hais plaub yuav luam xa Tsab Ntawv Thov, tej ntaub ntawv rhais nrog, 
thiab qhov OFP los sis Ntawv Yuam Muaj Rooj Sib Hais mus rau tus sheriff, uas yog tus 
yuav muab cov ntaub ntawv no nqa mus cec rau tus Neeg Raug Foob. 

  If your Petition has a claim of child abuse, the court may report it to the appropriate 

authority. 
Yog tias koj Tsab Ntawv Thov muaj lus aws txog kev tsim txom me nyuam, lub tsev hais 
plaub yuav ceeb toom mus rau tus muaj cai tsim nyog. 

Should You Answer All of the Questions? 
Koj Puas Yuav Tau Teb Tag Nrho Cov Nqe Lus Nug? 

Every question on the Petition (OFP102) and in the Guide & File “Protective Orders” 
interview is important. The judicial officer uses your information to decide whether to issue an 
OFP. 
Txhua nqe lus nug hauv Tsab Ntawv Thov (OFP102) thiab Kev Coj & Zwm ntawm "Cov 
Ntawv Kev Yuam Tiv Thaiv" tseem ceeb. Tus kws phua txim yuav siv koj cov ncauj lus mus 
txiav txim seb puas yuav muab qhov OFP.

If you choose not to provide all of the information, the judicial officer may not be able to grant 
your request and issue an OFP. 
Yog ias koj xaiv tsis teb tag nrho cov ncauj lus no, tus kws phua txim yuav tsis muaj peev xwm 
ua tau raws li koj thov thiab muab tsis tau qhov OFP. 

 

 

      What if an Address Changes? 

 
Yuav Ua Li Cas Yog Qhov Chaw Nyob Hloov? 

 

If there is an OFP in effect, you must let  
court administration and  
local law enforcement know where the new address is  
when the address changes RIGHT AWAY. 

Yog tias yeej muaj ib qho OFP ua hauj lwm tam sim no lawm, koj yuav tsum qhia  
TAM SIS rau lub chaw khiav tsev hais plaub paub thiab  
cov tub ceev xwm hauv zos kom paub qhov chaw nyob tshiab  
thaum twg hloov kiag qhov chaw nyob.

• 

If the new address is not confidential, you can use the Notice of Change of Address form 
(OFP801), available online at http://mncourts.gov/GetForms.aspx?c=17&f=329.  
Yog tsis ceev zoo cia qhov chaw nyob tshiab, koj siv tau Tsab Ntawv Faj Seeb Txog 
Chaw Nyob Hloov (OFP801), uas muaj nyob saum huab cua ntawm 
http://mncourts.gov/GetForms.aspx?c=17&f=329.  

If the new address is confidential, please use the Confidential Address/Phone Request 
form (OFP107) instead (http://mncourts.gov/GetForms.aspx?c=17&f=318). 
Yog tias yuav tau ceev zoo cia qhov chaw nyob tshiab, thov siv Tsab Ntawv Thov Ceev 
Zoo Cia Chaw Nyob/Xov Tooj (OFP107) 
(http://mncourts.gov/GetForms.aspx?c=17&f=318).
