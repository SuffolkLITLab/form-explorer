INSTRUCTIONS 
Conciliation Court Affidavit of Noncompliance 

Important Notices and Resources 

The Court has forms and instructions, for some types of cases, as a general guide to the court process. 
These instructions  explain the steps in more detail and answer common questions but are not a full 
guide to the law. Court employees may be able to give general information on court rules and 
procedures, but they cannot give legal advice.  

Have questions about court forms or instructions? 

• Visit www.MNCourts.gov/SelfHelp 
• Call the Statewide Self-Help Center at 651-435-6535 

Not sure what to do about a legal issue or need advice? 

• Talk with a lawyer 
• Visit www.MNCourts.gov/Find-a-Lawyer.aspx  

Helpful materials may be found at your public county law library. For a directory, see  
http://mn.gov/law-library/research-links/county-law-libraries.jsp . For more information, contact court 
administration or call the Minnesota State Law Library at 651-297-7651. 

Forms You Will Need 

•  Affidavit of Noncompliance (CCT107); and 
•  Affidavit of Service - Combined (SOP105). 

Forms are available online at www.mncourts.gov/forms.    

What You Need to Do 

1.  Complete the court forms, following all of the steps in these instructions. 

2.  Serve the other party with a copy of the Affidavit of Noncompliance. 

3.  File the forms with the Court (either electronically  or at the courthouse).

“Conciliation Court” is also known as "small claims"  court.  General civil  claims  of $15,000  or 
less can be filed in conciliation  court.  This court allows people to bring their legal claims without the 
expensive costs or complicated legal procedures of district court.  

“Settlement agreement” is when the parties agree to resolve the dispute without having the judicial 
officer decide their dispute. 

“Judgment” is a word that means “the judicial  officer’s decision” or “the judicial  officer’s order.” 

“Entry” or “Entered” is the word used when a judgment is filed in the case by the Court 
Administrator.  This usually starts the timeframe when a judgment can be appealed. 

When can you use the Affidavit of Noncompliance in Conciliation Court? 

The Affidavit of Noncompliance may be used ONLY IF all  of the below are true for your case:   

•  Your case was in conciliation  court (you cannot use these forms if your case was in district 

court);  

•  The parties signed a settlement agreement;  
•  The settlement agreement is part of the court record;  
•  The settlement agreement and/or court order says the Court may enter a judgment without 
a hearing if the agreement is violated and one of the parties files an affidavit explaining how 
the agreement was violated; AND 

•  One of the parties violated the terms of the settlement agreement. 

If you are not sure if all  of the above are true for your case, you should talk to an attorney 
(mncourts.gov/Help-Topics/Find-a-Lawyer.aspx).

The Signature Block 

Date and sign the Affidavit of Noncompliance, and print contact information in the blanks under the 
signature line.  When you sign the Affidavit of Noncompliance, you are signing under penalty of 
perjury.  This means you are saying that everything in the form is true and correct; if you know 
something in the form is not true when you sign it, you could be found guilty of the crime of perjury 
(see Minn. Stat. § 609.48,  revisor.mn.gov/statutes/cite/609.48).

Step 2 
Make Copies of the Affidavit of Noncompliance (CCT107) 

1.  Make one copy of the signed Affidavit of Noncompliance form for each party in your case, 

including  one copy for yourself.   

2.  The original  will be filed with court administration (later in step 4).   

 

Step 3 
Serve the Affidavit of Noncompliance (CCT107) on the other parties. 
Complete the Affidavit of Service – Combined (SOP105). 

The other parties must receive a copy of the Affidavit of Noncompliance that you plan to file with 
the court.  This is called “service of process.”  The Affidavit of Noncompliance form can be served 
personally (hand delivered to the other party) or served by mail.   

Papers CANNOT be served on legal holidays as defined in Minn. Stat. § 645.44,  subd. 5 
(revisor.mn.gov/statutes/cite/645.44). 

The Affidavit of Noncompliance can be served by any of the following: 

Who Can Serve 

•  The sheriff; 
•  Another adult; or 
•  You. 

To serve the papers personally: 

The server hands the Affidavit of Noncompliance to the other party or a person “of suitable age and 
discretion” at the other party’s home if that person lives with the other party. 

To serve the papers by mail: 

The server places one copy of the Affidavit of Noncompliance in an envelope. The server writes your 
return address and the last known address of the other party on the front of the envelope. The 
server places the correct amount of postage on the envelope. The server may want to take the 
envelope to the post office to be weighed to make sure they have the right amount of postage.  The 
envelope should be mailed using first class mail.   

After the paperwork is served on the other party (not before), the person who served the Affidavit 
of Noncompliance must fill out the Affidavit of Service - Combined (SOP105).

What to Expect Next… 

The judicial  officer will review the Affidavit of Noncompliance and may sign an Order for Judgment if 
the judicial officer decides the requirements to enter judgment have been met. If the judicial officer 
signs an Order for Judgment, Court Administration  will provide a copy of the Notice and the Order 
for Judgment and Judgment to all parties of the case.   

Sometimes the judicial  officer may schedule a hearing before signing an Order for Judgment. If this 
happens, you will  get a Notice of Hearing from Court Administration instructing when to appear in 
court.
