HEALTH CARE EXPENSES 
Minn. Stat. § 518A.41, subd. 17 

se these forms to challenge the amount if: 

•  You have received a “Notice of Intent to Collect Unreimbursed or Uninsured Health Care 
Expenses and Request for Payment” or “Notice of Motion, Motion and Affidavit to 
Collect Unreimbursed or Uninsured Health Care Expenses” from the other parent asking 
you to pay him/her for your share of the health care expenses for your joint child(ren)  
OR 

•  You have received notice from the County Child Support Agency regarding collection of 

unreimbursed or uninsured health care expenses, AND 

•  You believe that the amount stated in the Notice or Motion is incorrect or you want a 

monthly payment schedule, and you have not been able to reach an agreement with the 
other party or the county. 

efinition of terms: 
Health care expenses 
 
Joint child 
 
Uninsured health care expenses 

Include both medical and dental expenses of the joint child. 

The dependent child of both you and the other parent as listed in the child support order. 

Reasonable and necessary medical and dental  expenses incurred if the joint child is not 
covered by a health plan, including over the counter medications. 

Unreimbursed health care expenses 

Reasonable and necessary medical and dental expenses not covered by the child’s health 
plan,  that  include,  but  are  not  limited  to,  deductibles,  co-payments,  expenses  for 
orthodontia and prescription eyeglasses and contacts. Unreimbursed medical expenses do 
not include the cost of premiums or over-the-counter medications.

The forms you have prepared can be served by any of the following: 

ho Can Serve 

•  The sheriff; 
•  Another adult; or 
•  You. 

ersonal Service 

The server hands the other party (or their attorney, if they have one) one copy of your completed 
forms and attachments, or leaves them at the other party’s place of residence with some person 
of suitable age and discretion who also lives at the same residence.  Forms must be hand-
delivered  at least 14 days before the hearing date.  NOTE: If responding to the other party’s 
motion, the forms must be personally served at least 5 days before the hearing date. 

ervice by Mail 

The server mails a copy of your forms to the other party (or their attorney, if they have one) by 
first class U.S. mail at least 17 days before the hearing date. The server must drop the letter in 
the mailbox or give it to the postal worker.  NOTE: If responding to the other party’s motion, the 
forms must be mailed at least 8 days before the hearing date. 

ARNING:  The  Court  may  ignore  your  response  if  you  do  not  serve  and  file  the 

responsive papers on time.

ourt Fees 

There is no motion filing fee for this motion.  However, if you did not pay an initial filing fee 
when this case first began, you will be asked by court administration to pay it now. Court 
administration will be able to tell you if you have paid the initial filing fee. 

f a fee is owed and you cannot afford to pay the fee, a judge may waive it under certain 
circumstances.  Ask Court Administration for the In Forma Pauperis (IFP) (Fee Waiver) forms 
or download the forms at http://www.mncourts.gov/GetForms.aspx?c=19&p=69. You need to 
fill out the forms and sign them. Your fee waiver forms will be reviewed by a judge who will 
determine whether you must pay the filing fee or if it can be waived.  If the judge does not sign 
the form which waives the fee, you must be prepared to pay the filing fee.
