INSTRUCTIONS 
Petition for Restoration of Firearm and 
Ammunition Rights (FIR202) 

Use this form when you are not eligible to possess firearms and ammunition due to a civil 
commitment.  If you are not eligible to possess firearms and ammunition due to a criminal 
conviction or juvenile adjudication for a crime of violence, use forms FIR101 and FIR102.  

Forms you will need for this process:  

•  Petition for Restoration of Firearm and Ammunition Rights (FIR202) 
•  Affidavit of Service (FIR203)    

What you Need to Do 

1.  Complete the court forms, following all of the steps in these instructions. 
2.  Serve copies of the Petition.   
3.  File the forms with the Court (either electronically or at the courthouse). 

Important Notices and Resources 

he Court has forms and instructions, for some types of cases, as a general guide to the court 

process. These instructions explain the steps in more detail and answer common questions, but are 
not a full guide to the law. Court employees may be able to give general information on court rules 
and procedures, but they cannot give legal advice.

 General Information About Filing a  
Petition for Restoration of Firearm and Ammunition Rights (FIR202) 

he information contained in this document is not intended as legal advice but as a general 
guide to you to explain the legal process.  If you do not understand any of these procedures, 
talk to an attorney.  Court staff cannot give legal advice.    

inn. Stat. § 624.713, subd. 4 allows individuals to ask the court to restore the right to possess 

firearms and ammunition after commitment resulting from a judicial determination that the 
person is mentally ill, developmentally disabled, mentally ill and dangerous, or chemically 
dependent. 

What information do I need to complete the Petition?  

he judge will need information about any civil commitment cases that resulted in the loss of 

firearm and ammunition rights to determine to grant your petition. 

he judge may want details from the relevant civil commitment case(s). You may want to bring 
the following to the hearing: a copy of any adjudication/commitment order; a copy of any petition 
that  sought  to  have  you  so  adjudicated  or  committed;  and  a  copy  of  any  order  showing  your 
discharge from commitment or restoration of mental competency. 

f you are not sure if this applies to you, talk with an attorney.  

t is recommended that you get the following:  

1.  Register of Action: Request a copy of the Register of Action (ROA) for the case that 

resulted in the loss of the right to possess a firearm. If you were civilly commited under 
different cases, each of these commitments could have caused you to lose your right to 
possess a firearm.  Make sure to request and attach to your Petition a ROA for each Civil 
Commitment cases that caused you to lose your right to possess a firearm. 

f you have more than one Civil Commitment case in multiple counties and/or judicial 
districts and are unsure which case caused you to lose this right, you should talk to an 
attorney for legal advice.  

he ROA can be printed at a MN county courthouse through the Records Center or the 

Minnesota Public Access terminals. There is a court copy fee for the ROA.

OTICE: Even if the judge grants your Petition, you may still be prohibited from possessing 
firearms and ammunition under other applicable state or federal laws. We strongly encourage 
you to get legal advice and not assume you may lawfully possess a firearm even if your Petition 
is granted.  

Where should I file the Petition?  

here are a few issues to keep in mind when deciding where (County) to file your case and 

whether to file within your existing case or to start a new Civil case.  There may be a filing fee to 
file this Petition. Please consider the following issues to help decide where to file and in which 
case.  

ne Case with Civil Commitment: If you were civilly commited in only one case, you may 
choose to file this Petition in that case. Filing within that specific case will make sure that any 
order restoring your rights is available in that case and is communicated to state and federal 
justice partners who are responsible for determining your eligibility to possess firearms. There is 
no filing fee when you file into your Civil Commitment case. If you choose to open a new Civil 
case then you will pay the filing fee in that county.  

ultiple Cases: If you have civil commitments in different cases, each of these Civil 

Commitments could have caused you to lose your right to possess a firearm.  Make sure to list in 
your Petition all of the Civil Commitment cases that caused you to lose your right to possess a 
firearm.   
 

•  Cases in the same Judicial District – If all of your cases are in the same Judicial 

District, even if in different counties (for example, all the cases are in the 10th Judicial 
District but are in Anoka County and Washington County) then you may:

o  File one Petition in the county where one of the cases is located, or where you 

live, as a new Civil case. Each Civil Commitment case will need to be included in 
the body of the Petition. There is a filing fee to open a new Civil case.

Step 3 

Serve the Petition for Restoration of Firearm and Ammunition Rights 
(FIR202) and complete the Proof of Service (FIR203) 

innesota law does not state who MUST be served a copy of the Petition. However, you may 

want to serve any agencies that may have an interest in whether or not you have your rights 
restored. This could include:  

•  County Attorney’s Office  
•  County Sheriff’s Office  
•  County Human Services 
•  Minnesota Department of Human Services 

f you are unsure who to serve, you should talk with an attorney.  

his is called “service of process.”  You must have someone else who is over the age of 18 

and not involved in the case serve the papers by mail as follows:  
 

•  Someone else (not you) places one copy of Petition in an envelope. The server writes 

your return address and the address of the agency on the front of the envelope. The server 
places the correct amount of postage on the envelope. The server may want to take the 
envelope to the post office to be weighed to make sure they have the right amount of 
postage.  The envelope should be mailed using first class mail.   

fter the paperwork is mailed to the other party (not before),  the person who served the 

Petition for Restoration of Firearm and Ammunition Rights (FIR202) must fill out the Proof of 
Service (FIR203) form.
