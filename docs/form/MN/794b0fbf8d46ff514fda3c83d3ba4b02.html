To be represented by an attorney. If you are the child, the child’s parent, or the child’s legal custodian, the Court 
may appoint an attorney to represent you if the Court determines that you qualify financially and that the 
appointment is appropriate. If you are the parent or Indian custodian of an Indian child, the court is required to 
appoint an attorney to represent you if you qualify financially. The Court cannot appoint an attorney to represent 
you if the only allegation is that the child is a truant, unless out-of-home placement is being considered by the 
court. The Court may order a parent or legal custodian to reimburse some or all of the attorney’s fees.
