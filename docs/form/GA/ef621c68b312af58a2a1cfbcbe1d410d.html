Specific Instructions 

INSTRUCTIONS 

1. 

This form is to be used when a conservator requests leave to sell, rent, lease, or otherwise 
dispose of real or personal property at public or private sale pursuant to  
 

O.C.G.A.§29-3-35(c) or §29-5-35(c). 

 

 

 

 
 

With regard to paragraph 3 of the petition, a recent appraisal should be used if available; 
otherwise, a copy of the most recent ad valorem tax statement should be provided.  If the 
appraised value or tax value is higher than the selling price, explain the discrepancy on an 
additional sheet, and add a reference to this in paragraph 3 of the petition. 

This petition is to be filed by the conservator and not by a guardian.  A guardian ad litem 
must be appointed for the minor/ward. 

Service of the Petition and Notice on the minor/ward and guardian ad litem must be by 
personal  service.    If  requested  by  the  Petitioner(s)  and  approved  by  the  Court,  service 
may  be  made  by  registered  or  certified  mail  or statutory  overnight  delivery  with  return 
receipt  requested  and  with  delivery  restricted  to  addressee  only.    See  O.C.G.A.  §29-9-
4(e) and/or O.C.G.A. §15-9-17. 
 
 
According  to  Probate  Court  Rule  5.6  (A),  unless  the  court  specifically  assumes  the 
responsibility,  it  is  the  responsibility  of  the  moving  party  to  prepare the proper  citation 
and  deliver  it  properly  so  it  can  be  served  according  to  law.  Pages  after  4  which  are 
labeled  “Court”  are  to  be completed by  the  moving  party,  unless  otherwise  directed  by 
the court.
