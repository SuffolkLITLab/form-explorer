Accessing Auction Site 
 
Anyone can access the tax deed site to view 
 
property listings, but in order to gain full access to 
 
 
the auction website, and to place bids, you must 
log in with a user name and password. A 
registration link on the homepage will guide you 
 
through the process of creating a free account. 
 
When logged in as a registered user, you will have 
 
access to direct links to the Tax Collector, Property 
 
 
Appraiser, and street views of parcels associated 
 
with tax deeds. 
  
 
Participating in Auctions/Placing Bids & Deposits 
 
In order to place a bid on tax deed auctions, your 
 
deposit must be received on time: 
 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

•

 

 

 

 

•

Wire Payments need one business day
 
to clear
 
Cash and certified fund deposits may be
made at our office by 4:00PM the day
 
 
 
prior to the sale. Please have your
 
 
bidder number with you.
Bids cannot be placed until funds have settled, 
 
and show as “Available” in your bidder account. 
Even if a transaction has been made or approved, 
 
funds can’t be used until shown as available. 
 
 
 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Name on Title 
 
The site allows the use of multiple names in which 
 
 
to take title. This feature is accessible using the 
 
 
“Manage Name(s) on Title” section from your 
 
bidder account. While you may set up several 
different names and addresses for titles, you must 
select which name you would like to appear on a 
particular tax deed – this is how the buyer’s name 
 
 
will appear on the recorded document in the 
 
Official Records. 
  
It is the bidder’s responsibility to select and verify 
 
the name on title. No changes can be made to the 
 
tax deed after the sale. 
 
 
If you are the winning bidder: 
  
You will need to confirm your payment option at 
 
the end of the sale regardless of how payment will 
be made. Your final transaction cannot be 
 
 
completed until this has been done. 
  
 
 
Payment must be made by 1:00 the following 
 
 
business day after the auction. If it is not received 
by that time, your deposit will be forfeited, the 
 
property will be reset in another sale, and you may 
 
 
 
be parred from participating in future auctions. 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

There are several ways to begin searching tax deed records 
 

 

 

Researching Public Records Online 
 

 

 
 

1. Start at the auction site (https://lee.realtaxdeed.com) to view properties scheduled in upcoming
 

 

 

auctions. Click on a sale date in the calendar to view listings for that day. Links within the listings can be
 
followed for more records.
 

 

 

 

 

 

2. View tax deed files in the case management system (https://lee.realtdm.com/public/cases/list). Enter
 
the tax deed number obtained from the auction site (above). You can also search other criteria such as
sale date or party name.
 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 
These are the main ways to view tax deed files. Please note that in order for your research to be thorough, you 
 
will likely need to access other records, including the Official Records, Tax Collector, Property Appraiser, and 
 
real estate sources. The Clerk cannot assist with the determination of liens and encumbrances, and cannot 
 
provide legal advice.

WARNING: BUYER BEWARE! 
 
The Lee County Clerk sells tax deed properties “as is”. The Clerk and the Lee County Tax 
Collector do not guarantee clear title on tax deed sales. It is the buyer’s responsibility to 
research properties for liens, other encumbrances, structures, tenants, etc., that may affect title 
 
or use of the property. Bidders are urged to seek legal advice before purchasing tax deed 
property. 
 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 
We recommend reading the FAQs on the auction site at www.lee.realtaxdeed.com (top of the page) and 
www.leeclerk.org on the Property Sales page.
