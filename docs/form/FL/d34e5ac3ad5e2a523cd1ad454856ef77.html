INFORMATION SHEET 
 
AFFIDAVIT OF COMPROMISED IDENTITY 
 

1. Complete the Affidavit of Compromised Identity and have it notarized. If the information of the

 
person who used your information is unknown, please indicate unknown.

 

2. If arrested for Criminal Traffic or Misdemeanor offenses you need to go to the arresting to have
a fingerprint comparison made with the fingerprints taken at the time of arrest. The Lee County
Sheriff’s Office can assist with any Lee County Arrest; Cape Coral Police Department or Ft.
Myers Police Department can assist if the arrest was made by that agency. The Fingerprint
Examiner will provide documentation as to whether or not the fingerprints match. You will need
 
that document to attach to the Affidavit to submit to the court. If the print is insufficient to make
 
the comparison, the arresting deputy/officer would have to be contacted to make a visual
comparison. The Lee County Sheriff’s Department can also provide the Compromised Identity
Theft Form that needs to be submitted to Florida Department of Law Enforcement (FDLE).

3. If you were issued a traffic citation for either a criminal or non -criminal offense, or a Notice you 
may obtain a certified copy of the citation/Notice to Appear from the Clerk of Court for copies, 
refer to the Fee Schedule for the costs.
** NOTE** If the citation has a thumbprint for comparison please see step 2 of this process to 
have the fingerprint comparison made. If a thumbprint is not available, you need to contact the 
officer who issued the citation in order for a visual comparison done.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

4. Please provide a clear copy of your current Driver’s License/State Issued ID with a visible 
photo. The Florida Department of Highway Safety Motor Vehicles often requires a copy for 
comparison.

 

5. You must return this Affidavit and all other documents before your scheduled court date to: 

 

Clerk of the Circuit Court, PO Box 2365, Fort Myers, Florida 33902-2507. If original documents 
are mailed, the Affidavit must be notarized and you keep a copy. If this process is done after the 
case is disposed, the judge may require a hearing to be scheduled. Make sure the case 
number(s) or citations number(s) are on all corresponding documentation.

 

 

6. The Affidavit will be processed only if substantial information is included and attached. It will

 

 

 

then be presented to the State Attorney’s Office or the presiding judge for review and
recommendation of dismissal and/or nolle prosequi. The judge’s office may require that you
set a hearing to resolve this matter so please make sure you provide a phone number in the
Affidavit. The Law Enforcement’s further identification document determining the identity of the
fingerprints needs to be attached to the Affidavit. If a determination of false identity cannot be
made at this time, you may be summoned to appear in court for further identification.
 
** Note ** You are required to attend any pending court appearance for the cases in question
until a final resolution of the identity affidavit has been determined.
 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 
7. If you have any questions regarding the completion procedures for the attached Affidavit of

 

 

Compromised Identity, please do not hesitate to contact our office at (239) 533-5000 between
the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m., Monday thru Friday.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

   

 
 

 
 

 
More information regarding all types of Identity Theft is available on Florida Department of 
 
Law Enforcement (FDLE) Websites 
 
 
In some instances of identity theft, a victim may be faced with a criminal record for a crime he 
or  she  did  not  commit.  The  Florida  Department  of  Law  Enforcement  (FDLE)  can  provide  a 
 
Compromised  Identity  Review  (based  on  a  fingerprint  comparison  of  state  criminal  history 
 
files) to determine what, if any, criminal history belongs to you, and if any arrest records have 
 
been  falsely  associated  with  you  as  a  result  of  someone  using  your  identity.  If  a  fingerprint 
check  determines  you  are  an  identity  theft  victim,  FDLE  will  work  with  local  law enforcement 
 
agencies to attempt to clear fraudulent data from the criminal history files and provide you with 
 
a Compromised Identity Certificate. 
 
 
The FDLE has developed a program to assist with identity theft claims and those individuals who 
 
are concerned that their personal identifiers may have been used in an arrest record contained 
 
within the Florida Computerized Criminal History (CCH) files. Individuals can review their criminal 
history files and obtain other information by using the links provided. 
 
 
  
http://www.fdle.state.fl.us/compid/ 
 
http://www.myfloridalegal.com/identitytheft
