Guidelines 

Implementation of Electronic Citation Program 

The  implementation of an  Electronic  Citation Program  (e-Citation)  is  authorized  in  any  county, 
except  Cook  County,  within  the  State  of Illinois  by  the  Illinois  State  Police.  The  ISP  may 
implement  the  approved  electronic  citation  program  using  an  8  ½"  x  11"  citation  form,  which 
must be accepted for filing by circuit clerks in the remaining  101  Illinois counties. 

The implementation of an e-Citation program by local law enforcement may be authorized in any 
county upon the  submission of a written request from  each law enforcement agency to the Chief 
Judge of the Circuit (Chief Judge) and the Clerk of the Circuit Court (Circuit Clerk). 

The  following  criteria  shall  be  met  by  each  vendor  and  e-Citation  program  pnor  to 
implementation being authorized by the Circuit Clerk: 

D  The  Circuit  Clerk  has  been approved  and  is  actively  participating  in the  Automated 
Disposition  Reporting  Program. 
In  the  event  that  the  Automated  Disposition 
Reporting  Program  is  suspended  after  an  e-Citation  program  is  implemented, 
appropriate procedures must be implemented to ensure proper reporting of disposition 
information to the recipient agencies during the suspension. 

D  The  citation  form  and  format  shall  meet  the  minimum  standards  for  electronic 
citations approved  by  the  Conference of Chief Circuit Judges  (Conference) and filed 
with the Clerk of the Supreme Court pursuant to Supreme Court Rule 552. 

D  Identify the vendor performing the electronic citation functions and include a copy of 
each  page  of the  citation  form  indicating  the  type  of paper  (bond  or  thermal)  to  be 
used. 

D  The  application  for  court  supervision  and/or  traffic  safety  school  for  counties which 
have  been authorized to  accept supervision by  mail pursuant to  Supreme  Court Rule 
529(c)  must  meet  the  requirements  of Supreme  Court  Rules  and  the  Traffic  Safety 
Program Standards adopted by the Conference. 

D  Each citation shall  be  filed  with the  Circuit Clerk and  contain the  signature(s)  of the 
issuing  officer  and,  when  required,  the  violator.  Electronic  filing  and  electronic 
signatures may be applied only in counties authorized by the Supreme Court. 

D  Data and  document  exchange  fonnats  may  be  implemented  at  the  discretion  of the 

Circuit Clerk.

PRINTING and DISPLAY INSTRUCTIONS 

The  attached  electronic  citation,  hereafter  referred  to  as  the  "e-Citation,"  document  is  a 
copy  which  does  not  purport  to  reflect  either  spacing  or  size  of type  and  therefore  the 
following  is provided. 

(1)  DIMENSIONS 

The  overall  dimension  of the  uniform  e-Citation form  may  conform  to  the  paper 
citations filed  with the  Clerk of the  Supreme  Court which may  be  printed  on 8  ½ 
x  11  paper, or printed as a continuous form  approximately 4½ inches in width. 

If 8  ½  x  11  paper  is  used,  a  two-column  format  is  required.  The  citation  shall 
appear  on  approximately  the  left  4½  inches  of the  page.  The  instructions  to  the 
violator shall appear on the right side of the page. 

If a  continuous  form  is  used,  the  citation  shall  appear  first,  followed  by  the 
instructions  to  the  violator  or  the  instructions  may  appear  on  the  reverse  side  of 
the  citation.  This  format  shall  be used for  each copy  of the  e-Citation required  to 
be  printed as  provided in Number 3,  below. 

(2)  DEFINITION(S) 

The  definition  of "paper" for  an  e-Citation  must be  in  compliance  with  Supreme 
Com1 Rule and Illinois law and have a minimum of 20-year life quality.
