Re: 

Electronic Civil  Law Citation and Complaint Form 

The  Conference of Chief Circuit Judges, at its  meeting on  September  16,  2022,  approved  changes to 
the Electronic Civil Law Citation and Complaint Form (e-Civil Law Citation).  The approved updates 
are  part  of the  procedural  changes  resulting  from  the  Public  Act  101-0593  which  authorized 
recreational use of cannabis effective January  I, 2020.  Section 3.5 of the Drug Paraphernalia Control 
Act  was  removed  from  the  citation  which  originally  contained  violations  for  possession  of drug 
paraphernalia.  The  Printing  Instructions  have  also  been  updated  to  allow  Circuit  Court  QR  codes, 
violator·s email  address and  mobile number and  are included with this filing. 

On behalf of the Conference of Chief Circuit Judges,  I hereby submit the approved citation form  and 
printing instructions to your office for filing pursuant to Supreme Court Rule  552.  The new form  and 
printing instructions are effective January  I, 2023. 

Agencies must contact their electronic citation vendor to notify them of the changes as soon as possible 
to ensure the new citations are available no  later than July  I, 2023. 

Effective July  I, 2023, all  Electronic Civil Law Citations issued by any law enforcement agency must 
comply with the new  form  filed herein.

PRINTING and DISPLAY INSTRUCTIONS 

The attached electronic Civil Law Citation,  hereafter referred to as  the "e-Civil Law Citation," is  a  copy 
which does not purport to reflect either spacing or size of type and therefore the following is  provided. 

(1)  DIMENSIONS 

overall  dimension  of the  uniform  e-Civil  Law Citation  may  conform  to  the  paper citations 
filed  with  the  Clerk  of the  Supreme  Court,  may  be  printed  on  8  ½  x  11  paper,  or  printed  as  a 
continuous form  approximately 4½ inches in width. 

If 8  ½  x  11  paper  is  used,  a  two-column  format  is  required.  The  citation  shall  appear  on 
approximately the left 4½ inches of the page.  The  instructions to the violator shall appear on the 
right side of the page. 

If a  continuous  form  is  used,  the  citation  shall  appear  first,  followed  by  the  instructions  to  the 
violator,  or the  instructions may appear on the  reverse  side  of the  citation.  This format  shall  be 
used for each copy of the e-Civil Law Citation required to be printed as  provided in these Printing 
Instructions. 

(2)  DEFINITION(S) 

The definition of "paper" for  an e-Civil Law Citation must comply with Supreme Court Rule and 
Illinois law and have a minimum of20-year life quality.
