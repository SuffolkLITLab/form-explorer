Instructions to Defendant: 
 

A.  This form must be completed and filed with the Clerk of the Court on or before the time 

stated in the Summons for the trial. 

B.  If you have a claim against Plaintiff which is part of the same transaction or   

occurrence (which means the same event or circumstance) of Plaintiff’s claim against you 
and your claim does not exceed $4,000, you must state your claim by writing in paragraph 
1. Do not include interest, costs, and any damages under K.S.A. 60-2610 when calculating 
whether your claim exceeds $4,000.           
 

C.  You must write your demand for judgment in paragraph 2A, 2B, or both of Defendant’s 

Claim above.   
 

D.  If your claim exceeds $4,000, you may state your claim and demand for judgment on this 
form. If the value of your claim exceeds $4,000, the judge must decide whether you may 
pursue your entire claim or only the portion not exceeding $4,000 in small claims. 

E.  If your claim exceeds $4,000 and the judge decides that you may only file a portion of your 

claim, you have three alternatives: 
 

small claims; or 

1.  Tell the judge that you wish to file your entire claim in a different case outside of 

2.  Request judgment for the portion of your claim that does not exceed $4,000 and tell 
the  judge  that  you  wish  to  file  a  different  case  outside  of  small  claims  for  any 
amount over $4,000; or 

3.  Request judgment for the portion of your claim that does not exceed $4,000 and 

waive your right to file a different case.   

F.  After completing this form, you must sign it under penalty of perjury.
