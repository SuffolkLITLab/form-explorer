In  order  to  file  a  Petition  for  the  restoration  of  your  driving 
privileges, you must have been declared an habitual offender.  You 
must retain the Court order for restoration proceedings. 

 
•	  As  an  Habitual  Offender  under  21  Del.C.  §  2802,  your  driving 
privileges have been suspended pursuant to 21 Del.C. § 2809. 

 
•	  Subsection  (4)  of  §  2809  of  Title  21  permits  the  filing  of  a  petition 
 
for  early  restoration  of  driving  privileges  after  a  full  three  (3)  years 
has elapsed since you were declared as an habitual offender.   The 

 

 

 

 

   

 

 

 
Court  will  consider  your  Petition  if  all  fines  and  costs  have  been 
paid,  there  have  been  no  driving  violations  during  the  three  (3) 
years  since  you  were  declared  an  habitual  offender,  and  the 
Department of Justice has reviewed and approved the request.  

 
•	 

If a period of five (5) years has elapsed since you were declared an 
habitual  offender,  the  Department  of  Justice  does  not  need  to 
review your petition, however; the other conditions still apply.  File 
 
your  paperwork  directly  with  the  Clerk’s  Office  of  the  Court  of 
Common Pleas requesting your driving privileges be restored. 

 

 
•	 

If filing the Petition in Kent or Sussex Counties, you should contact 
the Clerk’s Office in that County for instructions.   

Kent County:  1-302-735-3900  Sussex  County:  1-302-858-5730
