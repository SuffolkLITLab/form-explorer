It is a court order that helps protect people from being abused. Can I get a restraining order? If you are a person 65 years or older or a dependent adult, you can ask for a restraining order if you have been or are being: Ł Physically abusedŁ Financially abusedŁ Mentally or emotionally abused Ł Neglected Ł Abandoned or abducted Ł Isolated, orŁ Deprived by a caregiver of goods or services you needed to avoid harm or suffering How will the order help me? The court can order a person to : Ł Not physically abuse, har ass, hit, or threaten you Ł Not contact or go near you, and Ł Not have a gun Judicial Council of California www. courts. ca. gov Revised January 1, 2012, Optional Form Welfare & Institutions Code, § 15657. 03 You can also ask for protection for people who live with you and family members. These instructions cannot cover all of the questions that may arise in a particular case. If you do not know what to do to protect your rights, you should see a lawyer. Who can apply for an elder or dependent adult abuse restraining order? In addition to the elder or dependent adult, the following persons may apply for a restraining order on behalf of the elder or dependent adult: Ł A conservator or trustee of the elder or dependent adult Ł An attorney in fact of an elder or dependent adult who acts within the authority of the power of attorney Ł A person appointed as a gu ardian ad litem for the elder or dependent adult Ł Any other person legally authorized to seek such relief. How much does it cost? There is no fee for filing a request for a restraining order. The court can make the person who loses the case pay all the court fees and the lawyer™s fees for the other party. You do not need to pay a fee for service of the order. A sheriff or marshal will serve th e order for free. Or you may arrange for service by a regist ered process server or a private party and pay any fee that is charged. What forms do I need to get the order? You must fill out all of Form EA 100, Request for Elder or Dependent Adult Abuse Restraining Orders, and Form CLETS 001, Confidential CLETS Information. If you need attachments, you may use Form MC 025, Attachment. You must also fill out items 1 and 2 on Form EA 109, Notice of Court Hearing, and items 1, 2, and 3 on Form EA 110, Temporary Restraining Order. Where can I get these forms? You can get the forms from legal publishers or on the Internet at www. courts. ca. gov. You also may be able to find them at your local courthouse or county law library. What do I need to do to get the order? You must go to the superior court in the county where the abuse took place or the person to be restrained lives. At the court, ask where you should file your request for a restraining order. (A self help center or legal aid association may be able to assist you in filing your request. ) At the court, give your forms to the clerk of the court. The clerk will give you a hearing date on the Notice of Court Hearing form, and if your request for immediate orders is granted, a copy of the Temporary Restraining Order signed by a judicial officer. How soon can I get the order? ŁIf you ask for a temporary restraining order, the court will decide within 24 hours whether or not to make the order. Sometimes the court decides sooner. Ask whether you should wait or come back later to get the signed Notice of Court Hearing and Temporary Restraining Order . Do I have to go to court? Yes. Go to court on the date the clerk gives you. Do I need a lawyer? Do I need to bring a witness to the court hearing? Witnesses are not required, but it helps to have more proof of the abuse than just your word. You can bring: Ł Witnesses Ł Written statements from witnesses made under oath Ł Photos Ł Medical or police reports Ł Damaged property Ł Threatening letters, e ma ils, or telephone messages Can I bring someone with me to court? Yes. You can bring someone to sit with you during the hearing. But that person cannot speak for you in court. Only you or your lawyer (if you have one) can speak for you. Rev. The restrained person can be arrested and charged with a crime. Will I see the restrained person at the court hearing? If the person comes to the hearing, yes. But that person does not have the right to speak to you. If you are afraid, tell the court officer. What if I don't speak English?
