6 Grounds for Issuance of a Gun Violence Restraining Order

I have reasonable cause to believe both of the following are true:

a. The Respondent poses a significant danger in the near future of causing personal injury to themself or another 
person by having in their custody or control, owning, purchasing, possessing, or receiving a firearm (gun), a 
firearm part (any receiver, frame, or unfinished receiver or frame as defined in Penal Code section 16531),  
ammunition, or a magazine.

b. A gun violence restraining order is necessary to prevent personal injury to Respondent or to another person 

because less restrictive alternatives either have been tried and found to be ineffective, or have been 
determined to be inadequate or inappropriate for the current circumstances.
