Here is the form text we found: Every time your conservatee or ward moves to a new residence in California, you must, within 30 days of the date of the move, give written notice of the change to the court and, unless the court excuses you for good cause to prevent harm to the conservatee or ward, mail a copy of the notice to the attorney for the conservatee or ward; and (a) in a conservatorship, mail copies of the notice to the conservatee's spouse or registered domestic partner and the conservatee's relatives named in the petition for appointment of a conservator in your case (the conservatee's second degree relatives, or if there is no spouse, registered domestic partner, and second degree relatives, the persons named in Probate Code section 1821 (b) (1) - (4) as the conservatee's "deemed relatives") ; or (b) in a guardianship, mail copies of the notice to the ward's parents, any person who had legal custody of the ward when the first petition for appointment of a guardian was file in your case, the guardian of the ward's estate, and any person nominated as a guardian for the ward who was not appointed.
