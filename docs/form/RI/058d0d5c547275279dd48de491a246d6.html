Your Day in Court

An Introductory Guide to the Rhode Island Judiciary

Like the judge and court personnel, all parties 

play important roles in creating a positive and 

respectful environment in our courts. While in 

court, you have the right to be treated with 

fairness, respect, and dignity by everyone that 

you encounter. In return, you have an obligation 

to follow the court rules and to treat all those 

that you encounter with courtesy and respect.

Courtroom Etiquette

•  Be sure to make arrangements for the care of 

Parking

•  Do not bring food or drinks into the courthouse. 

Interpreters

Resources

•  Dress appropriately for court. Do not wear hats, 
T-shirts, halter tops, shorts, or torn jeans. Be  
certain that your clothing is neat and clean. 

•  You need to be on time for court. Failure to  
appear in a timely manner may result in a  
warrant for your arrest or other court orders 
against you. 

•  Arrive at the courthouse 30 minutes ahead 
of your scheduled court time. You will need 
this time to clear security and locate your 
courtroom.  

•  Turn off all pagers and cell phones upon  

entering your assigned courtroom. 

•  Cameras or other recording devices are not  
allowed in the courthouse without prior  
permission.  

•  Sit and wait quietly in the courtroom until your 
case is called. Do not talk, chew gum, read a 
book or newspaper. Watch and observe.  

•  Address the judge as “Your Honor” or “Judge” 

and stand when speaking to the judge. Do not  
approach the judge. 

Be Prepared

•  Bring all court notices and necessary paperwork 

with you to court. 

•  Upon entering the courthouse, you will be  
greeted and screened by the Capitol Police.  
Be prepared to clear security. Do not bring  
weapons into the courthouse. You will have  
to pass through a metal detector. You may be 
asked to empty your pockets and to remove  
your belt and/or shoes. 

young children. 

•  Your case may not be heard and finished in one 
day. It may need to be continued or postponed. 
You may need to return to court on several 
occasions before your case in concluded.  

•  Please note that some Family Court hearings are 
confidential and are closed proceedings. You 
may be asked to leave the courtroom.  

•  Be prepared to pay court fines when ordered to 

do so.  

•  Be prepared to pay filing fees if you are filing a  

petition or a complaint.

•  If you do not speak fluent English and need an  
interpreter, please contact the Office of Court  
Interpreters by telephone at (401) 222-8710 or 
via email at interpreterfeedback@courts.ri.gov, 
prior to your scheduled court date. Court phone 
numbers are located on the back side of this 
brochure.  

•  If you are in need of a sign language interpreter 
or assistive listening equipment, please call the 
court Clerk’s Office before your scheduled  
hearing to make these arrangements. 

Disability

•  If you have a disability and are in need of  

information regarding access to the courthouse, 
please call the court where your case will be 
heard prior to your scheduled court date. Court 
phone numbers are listed on the back side of 
this brochure.  

•  If you cannot use the elevator, ask the Capitol 

Police for directions to the stairway.

•  Free parking is available at some courthouses 

but not others, so plan accordingly.  

•  Garrahy Judicial Complex: Private 

lots and garages for a fee and on-street 
parking (most with meters)

•  Licht Judicial Complex: Private lots and 
garages for a fee and on-street parking 
(most with meters)

•  Noel Judicial Complex: Free parking
•  McGrath Judicial Complex: Free parking
•  Murray Judicial Complex: On-street  

parking with meters

•  Rhode Island Traffic Tribunal: Free 

parking

•  General information booths are located on the 
first floor of each courthouse. This is where you 
can obtain information to assist you in locating 
your assigned courtroom.  
Information is also available at the Clerk’s Office 
for each court.  

•  In the Self-Help Center in the middle of the 

Judiciary’s home page, you will find a “How Do 
I?” section. Here you will find guidance on repre-
senting yourself, what court employees can do 
to help you, and what they cannot do. There is 
a section on frequently asked questions as well 
as a series of links for help in obtaining legal 
assistance. There are resources for getting a case 
expunged from your record, court forms, and 
how to look up a court rule, state law or conduct 
legal research.

You can obtain more information about 
state courts on the Judiciary website at 
WWW.COURTS.RI.GOV.
