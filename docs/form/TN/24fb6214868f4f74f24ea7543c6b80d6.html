Access & Visitation (A&V) Grant Recipients 
(Services performed October 1, 2020 – September 30, 2021) 

The organizations below receive Access and Visitation funds to address the needs of never-married parents, 
non-custodial parents and/or caretakers by assisting in resolution of issues concerning parenting and visitation. 
  
The funds allocated for the Tennessee’s Access and Visitation Program, under provisions of section 469B of 
the Title IV-D of the Social Security Act as amended, are to provide initiatives intended to address the needs of 
never-married parents, emphasizing parenting time issues of families and non-custodial parents in the Title IV-
D program. These initiatives may include efforts such as self-help centers, clinics for self-represented litigants, 
unbundled legal services, court ordered supervised visitation, and mediation programs. 

his funding is made possible through the U.S. Department of Health & Human Services Access and Visitation 
Mandatory  Grants  program  and  is  administered  by  the  Tennessee  Administrative  Office  of  the  Courts  in 
partnership with the State of Tennessee’s Department of Human Services.
