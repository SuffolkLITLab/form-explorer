IN THE SUPREME COURT OF TENNESSEE
AT NASHVILLE

IN RE ESTABLISHMENT OF FEES 
FOR THE OFFICE OF THE CLERK 
OF THE TENNESSEE SUPREME COURT

No. ADM2018-01171

ORDER

The Tennessee Supreme Court is authorized pursuant to T.C.A. § 8-21-501
to set the amount of court costs and other fees that the Clerk of the Supreme Court 
(“Clerk”)—who  also  serves  as  Clerk  of  the  Court  of  Appeals,    the  Court  of 
Criminal  Appeals,  and    the  Board  of  Judicial  Conduct—may  charge  for  the 
services provided by the Clerk’s office.

On  July  9,  2018,  the  Clerk  intends  to  implement  a  new  electronic  filing 
system.    The  rules  and  procedures  compiled  by  this  Court  in  preparation  for  the 
implementation of this new system necessitate a simpler method for charging costs 
and fees by the Clerk.  A study and review of costs and fees charged by the Clerk 
reflects that the costs and fees set out in the Appendix to this Order are reasonable 
and  will  permit  the  Clerk  to  continue  to  meet  his  obligations  in  accordance  with 
prior budgetary practices.

Pending  further  order  of  the  Court,  the  Supreme  Court  hereby  adopts  the 
schedule  of  costs  and  fees  set  out  in  the  Appendix  to  this  Order  which is
incorporated herein by reference.

Accordingly,  it  is  hereby  ORDERED  that  beginning  on  July  9,  2018 and 
until  further  order  of  the Court,  the  Clerk  shall  charge  the  costs  and  fees  for  the 
Clerk’s services as set out  in the  Appendix  to this Order. In addition to the costs 
and  fees  listed  in  the  Appendix,  the  Clerk  may  continue  to charge  all  fees  or 
charges otherwise authorized by statute or rule.

IT IS SO ORDERED.

PER CURIAM
