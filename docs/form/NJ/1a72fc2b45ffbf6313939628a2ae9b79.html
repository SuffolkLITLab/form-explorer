What is probation? 
Probation is a court sentence that 
allows certain clients to stay in the 
community under the supervision of a 
probation officer. Probation is a part of 
the New Jersey court system.

Probation is not parole. Parole monitors 
those released from state prison after 
they serve most of their sentences. 

Probation supervision involves working 
with a probation officer to successfully 
complete your probation term. It also 
involves reporting to the probation 
office, attending school, work, or 
job training programs and, when 
appropriate, participation in substance 
abuse and other treatment programs as 
well as community service.

The probation division also includes 
a child support enforcement unit that 
monitors child and spousal support 
orders. If you have a child support case, 
your probation officer will work with 
your child support caseworker to help 
you pay your support.

Am I expected to work while I am 
on probation?
Yes. In fact, clients often are required to 
have a job if they are on probation. 

Where can I pay my fines?
You can pay your fines in person at the 
local probation office or courthouse or  
by mail. 

How do I participate in community 
service? 
A community service sentence will require 
you to work at a non-profit organization, 
government agency and/or a self-
enrichment organization for a period of 
time decided by the court. Probation staff 
will make sure you are referred to a suitable 
community service worksite or alternative 
where you can complete hours.

What if I am unable to complete 
my community service? 
You should contact your local probation 
office for guidance and or options you 
might have if you feel as though you 
cannot complete community service. We 
will work to connect you and provide 
you with any alternatives that qualify for 
community service hours.  

Office of Probation Services Units
Probation is more than community 
supervision and enforcing court orders.

What can I expect while I am on 
probation? 
Probation officers, who are trained 
professionals, will work with you, your 
family, the court and community providers 
to help you accomplish your goals and 
stay arrest free.

Probation will begin with a meeting with 
your probation officer to develop a list of 
goals that will be used as a roadmap for 
successfully completing probation. This 
roadmap is your case plan.

A risk assessment tool will be used to 
develop the case plan, which could 
include drug testing, community service, 
home visits and setting up a payment plan 
for fines. Your probation officer will have 
regular contact with you in your home and 
at their office to assist you throughout  
this process.

What services can probation 
connect me with?
You will receive help and be connected 
to education, job training and placement, 
housing assistance, drug and alcohol 
treatment and counseling services, among 
other services, that are available in 
your community.

You also will be connected to information 
about job readiness, financial literacy, 
self-help organizations such as Narcotics 
Anonymous (NA), Alcoholics Anonymous 
(AA), and 12 steps and basic civics.
