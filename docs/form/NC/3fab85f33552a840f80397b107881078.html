It appears to the Court that the best interest of the Court and the defendant will be served by a presentence investigation and commitment of the defendant. 
The Division of Community Supervision and Reentry of the Department of Adult Correction has notified the Court that a presentence study may begin at the 
date and location designated below. Pursuant to G.S. 14-208.20(b) or with the consent of the defendant, and pursuant to G.S. 15A-1332, it is ORDERED that 
the defendant be committed to the custody of the Division of Community Supervision and Reentry for a presentence study for a period not to exceed ninety 
(90) days, as follows:

Date Commitment To Begin

Location Of Diagnostic Center

While the defendant is in custody of the Division of Community Supervision and Reentry, that division shall conduct a complete study of the defendant and 
as part of that study shall:

1.  a. inquire into the defendant’s previous delinquency, criminal experience, social background, capabilities and mental, emotional and physical health.

b.  report to the Court on the availability of resources or programs appropriate to the defendant, both inside and outside of the Division of Community

Supervision and Reentry and, in particular, in the area where the defendant resides.

c. make recommendations to the Court concerning the defendant.

2. determine whether the defendant is a sexually violent predator.

3. other matters:

Upon completion of such study or at the end of the 90-day period, whichever shall occur first, the Division of Community Supervision and Reentry shall release 
the defendant to the sheriff of the county named, who shall return the defendant to this Court for further proceedings. The Division of Community Supervision 
and Reentry shall forward copies of this study to the Clerk of Superior Court. The Clerk shall furnish copies to the district attorney and counsel for the 
defendant upon request.

The Court further ORDERS that the Division of Community Supervision and Reentry shall make a presentence investigation and forward a copy to the Director 
of the Diagnostic Center as set forth above.

The Clerk of Superior Court shall forward a copy of this Order to the district attorney, the Division of Community Supervision and Reentry and to counsel for the 
defendant.

ORDER OF COMMITMENT
It is ORDERED that the Clerk deliver three certified copies of this Presentence Commitment to the sheriff or other qualified officer and that the officer cause the 
defendant to be delivered with these copies to the custody of the diagnostic center named above, on the date that commitment is to begin as specified above.
