FINDINGS AND ORDER

The defendant named above was convicted of an offense in the above-captioned case(s) whereby his/her citizenship rights were 
forfeited. The undersigned hereby finds that the defendant has been discharged unconditionally from the court’s supervision on the date 
shown below, having completed any period of unsupervised probation or having satisfied the terms of the judgment of conviction, if no 
probation or imprisonment was imposed. Pursuant to this finding, the undersigned hereby Orders that the defendant’s citizenship rights 
that were forfeited solely as a result of the defendant’s conviction in the above-captioned case(s) are restored.

This Order restores some, but not all, of the rights forfeited as the result of your conviction in the case(s) listed above. This restoration 
applies only to rights forfeited as a result of the specific case(s) listed; it does not restore rights still forfeited as a result of other 
convictions.

NOTE TO DEFENDANT

If you have no other convictions for which your rights were forfeited, this Order does restore your rights to vote, to serve on a jury, and to 
run for or hold most (but not all) elected or appointed offices in the State. To vote, you must first register with your local board of elections. 
Information on registering to vote is available online at www.ncsbe.gov.

This Order does not: 
■ expunge, reverse, or conceal the record of conviction in the case listed above;
■ restore the right to possess a firearm;
■ restore a drivers license or other government-issued license that may have been revoked or suspended as a result of your conviction;
■ remove other consequences of conviction, like registration as a sex offender or a court’s finding of domestic violence offender status;
■ satisfy outstanding civil obligations arising from the criminal case, like attorney fees, restitution, or forfeitures;
■ prevent some licensing agencies or employers from considering your conviction in licensing or employment decisions; or
■ restore other rights or privileges not specifically excluded here.

Any questions about the rights that are or are not restored by this Order should be directed to an attorney or to the government agency 
with authority over the right or benefit in question (e.g., the local board of elections). The clerk of superior court cannot give you legal 
advice about rights that are or are not restored by this Order.
