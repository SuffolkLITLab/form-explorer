DEFINITIONS 

robate - The legal process of gathering and distributing the property of the 
estate, paying creditors’ claims, and formally transferring the possessions of 
the deceased to those persons who “inherit” them. 

eceased or decedent - The person who has died. 

state - The property (both real and personal) held by the decedent in 

his/her name alone or as tenants in common at the time of death. 

ill - A document representing the instructions of the deceased for 

distribution of the estate. 

egatee - Anyone named in a will who is to receive property. 

eir - A person entitled to share in a decedent’s estate where there is no 

will. 

nterested person - Any heir or legatee or creditor with a claim in excess 

of $500.00 that has not been barred or discharged. 

reditor - A person who has a claim against the estate. 

ersonal Representative - The person appointed by the Court to 

administer (i.e., to be in charge of) an estate. 

eal property - Any real estate owned by the person who has died, such as 

a home, vacation cottage, or vacant land. 

ersonal property - Everything other than real property, such as a car, 

furniture, jewelry, stocks, and bank accounts.

enants in common - Generally, where more than one person owns or
