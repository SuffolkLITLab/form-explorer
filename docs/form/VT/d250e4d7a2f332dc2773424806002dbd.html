  I understand that as a defendant in a criminal case, I have certain constitutional and statutory rights.  I understand that I
may plead NOT GUILTY to this offense, and that the State would then have to prove me guilty beyond a reasonable
doubt.  I understand that by pleading GUILTY or NOLO CONTENDERE I am giving up the following rights, and that the
Court may impose the same punishment as if I had plead NOT GUILTY, stood trial, and been convicted by a jury:

a. the right to appear before a judge to have my rights explained and be given a copy of the Information and

the Affidavit describing the charges against me;

b. the right to a trial by jury;
c. the right to question any witnesses testifying against me;
d. the right to present evidence and have witnesses brought to the Court for me at State expense;
e. the right to remain silent regarding the offense with which I am charged;
f. the right to appeal.

No threats or force have been used against me by anyone to try to make me plead guilty or nolo contendere. I plead
guilty or nolo contendere of my own choice, freely and voluntarily.  No promises have been made to me by anyone for the
State except those which are contained in the attached Notice of Plea Agreement.  I agree that there is a factual basis for my
plea of guilty or nolo contendere, supported by the affidavit.

I understand that by pleading GUILTY I admit that I committed the essential parts of the offense with which I have been
charged.  I understand that by pleading NOLO CONTENDERE I do not deny that I committed the essential parts of the
charge.  I further understand that a plea of GUILTY or NOLO CONTENDERE to a violation of Title 23 of the Vermont
Statutes Annotated may result in a suspension of my license or privilege to operate a motor vehicle in the State of
Vermont. I understand that, if I am not a citizen of the United States, a plea of GUILTY or NOLO CONTENDERE
may  have consequences of deportation or denial of United States citizenship.

I hereby ask the Court to accept my plea of
