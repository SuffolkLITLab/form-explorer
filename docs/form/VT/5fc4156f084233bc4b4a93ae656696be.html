APPENDIX F 

NOTICE TO HOMEOWNER  
RE: VERMONT HOMEOWNER ASSISTANCE PROGRAM 
 

You may be able to get help with past-due mortgage payments through the Vermont Homeowner 
Assistance Program (VHAP). VHAP may be able to help you avoid foreclosure by paying up to 
$30,000 in past-due mortgage payments, escrow charges, and other fees if you have suffered a 
financial hardship related to the COVID-19 pandemic. Financial hardships can include job loss, a 
reduction in income, or increased costs due to illness or the need to care for a family member.  

ou can apply for VHAP online at https://vermonthap.vhfa.org   

For advice about the program and help completing the application, contact the nearest local 
agency listed at https://www.vhfa.org/news/HAP/affiliates call the VHAP program at 833-
221-4208, or contact Vermont Legal Aid at 1-800-889-2047 or 
https://vtlawhelp.org/foreclosures-coronavirus 

f you inform the court that you have applied for VHAP funds, any judgment or sale in the 
foreclosure case will be put on hold (stayed) for 60 days while your application is 
reviewed, unless the Plaintiff files an objection and the court ends the stay. If you want to 
ask the court for a stay, you should fill out and sign the attached form, file it with the court, 
and send a copy to the plaintiff’s attorney.

 have not previously received VHAP funds for mortgage or mobile home loan payments on or 
after January 1, 2022. 

 experienced a financial hardship after January 21, 2020 associated with the COVID-19 
pandemic. 

 believe I meet the income guidelines for VHAP.
