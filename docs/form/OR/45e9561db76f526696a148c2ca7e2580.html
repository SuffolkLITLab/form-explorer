CSC Oral Exam Development 

he Language Access Services Section (LASS) of the National Center for State Courts (NCSC) 
develops and maintains valid and reliable oral performance examinations that can be used by state 
court systems to credential persons who wish to become spoken language court interpreters. 

he NCSC’s oral exams are developed to measure a candidate’s ability to faithfully and 
accurately interpret the range of English ordinarily used in courtrooms into another language, 
and to understand and interpret into English what is said by a native speaker of another 
language. 

ll NCSC oral exam development is supervised by a Test Development Manager and oral 
exams are developed by a team consisting of at least two specialists as described below: 

•  One applied linguist, preferably a practicing professional interpreter who possesses 

the highest credentials available in the field; and 

•  One theoretical, scholarly linguist who has the most formal academic training 

possible in the linguistics of the language. 

he NCSC’s oral exam content is based on transcripts from court proceedings that involve 
general legal concepts and procedures and on types of legal situations that require large 
volumes of interpreting services, representing a wide range of: 

•  Stages of legal proceedings (e.g., preliminary hearings, trials, sentencing); 

•  Types of proceedings and legal subjects heard in state courts (e.g., criminal, family, 

and civil courts); and  

 

•  Levels of proceedings (e.g., state, county, and, to a lesser degree, municipal courts). 

NCSC oral exams are developed in accordance with a Test Construction Manual, which 
ensures that the development of each oral exam part is standardized across languages.  

ll NCSC oral exams include an even distribution of scoring units, which are specific linguistic 

phenomena that interpreters must be able to deliver for a complete and accurate 
interpretation.  

n addition, all audio recordings developed as part of an NCSC oral exam are recorded by native 
speakers of English and of the non-English language, in accordance with the Test Construction 
 
Manual standards.

NCSC Oral Exam Rating 

ach NCSC oral exam is rated by at least two NCSC-approved raters. Raters include individuals 
with the highest level of applied linguistic credentials, including certified court interpreters, 
and individuals with extensive academic studies in the language.  

aters are trained in accordance with the NCSC’s Test Rating Manual, which provides 

guidelines for the oral examination rating process to ensure that all NCSC oral exams are rated 
in an objective, valid, and reliable manner for all languages. 

nder the NSCS’s standardized rating guidelines, raters are trained to be open to geographical 
and social variations in usage and to rate renditions provided by candidates in accordance with 
established rater scoring criteria and research processes.
