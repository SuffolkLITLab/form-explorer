WHAT IS A PARENTING PLAN? A  
Parenting Plan is a document that is  
used when you and the other parent  
of your child are not living in the same 
house.  A Parenting Plan states when 
your child will be with each parent  
and how decisions will be made. 

WHY WOULD I WANT A  
PARENTING PLAN? Having a  
parenting plan provides parents with 
specific details about the time they  
share with their children and who is 
responsible for important decisions.  
Oregon law requires parents (married  
or unmarried) to file a Parenting Plan  
with a request for a divorce or child 
custody order.  In addition, a parenting 
plan may help you meet the requirement 
of an agreement in writing if you intend  
to use the “parenting time credit” in 
calculating child support.  

HOW CAN I GET HELP TO  
CREATE A PARENTING PLAN?  
There are two parenting plan guides  
that have been developed to assist 
divorcing/separating parents.  The 
materials will help you create a plan  
that fits your family’s unique needs  
and your child’s age.  The Basic  
Parenting Plan Guide also tells  
you about other places where you  
can get help to create your parenting  
plan.   

WHICH GUIDE SHOULD I USE?  
The description that follows should 
help you decide which publication 
is right for you. 

THE BASIC PARENTING PLAN 
GUIDE. This guide includes: 
$  an introduction to the parenting 
plan process 
$  a list of questions a parent can ask 
to get started 
$  a chart showing ages and stages 
of development of children and 
sample schedules 
$  a fill-in-the blank form with 
instructions 
$ A fill-in-the-blank long distance form 
with instructions 
$  a resource list of books and other 
materials 
$  a glossary of words and definitions. 
THE SAFETY-FOCUSED 
PARENTING PLAN GUIDE.  
Available in English or Spanish.  For 
a parent who has safety concerns 
about parenting time (see other side 
of this brochure), this guide contains 
three different parenting plans: 
• Option A with supervised visitation
• Option B with unsupervised-no
overnight parenting time 
• Option C with unsupervised-
overnight parenting time
