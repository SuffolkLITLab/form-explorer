Basic Parenting Plan Guide 

COVER PAGE 

HAT IS A PARENTING PLAN? 

This page gives a basic introduction to the concept of a parenting plan. It gives parents 
links to several resources to help them write their parenting plan. It includes safety 
screening questions to help a parent decide whether to use the basic  parenting plan or 
the safety-focused parenting plan. 

OW DO I GET STARTED? 

This section lists questions for parents to ask themselves so they may write a parenting 
plan that fits their own family’s needs.  It will help guide them through the parenting plan 
form.  

ASIC PARENTING PLAN FORM 

This is the basic parenting plan form with check boxes and simple choices. It offers 
opportunities throughout to help a parent write a plan that fits his or her family’s needs. 
The form may be filled in on-line, saved, changed, emailed, or printed. A parent may 
print the blank form and fill it in by hand. 

ASIC PARENTING PLAN WORKSHEET 

This is the same basic parenting plan form with detailed instructions. 

DDITIONAL PAGES TO BASIC FORM 

These attachments give parents additional choices for writing a parenting plan to meet 
their family’s needs. 

ttachment 3(c) offers a place for parents to set out a different parenting time schedule 

for a child whose needs are different from the other child’s needs, or to include a 
different schedule for later, when the child’s needs have changed because they are  
older. 
Attachment 4.2 offers a place to add additional holidays or other days that may be 
special to a family. 
Attachment 9.5 offers a place to add more paragraphs to the parenting plan.

LANK CALENDAR 

Parents can use the blank calendar to work out an ongoing schedule or to create a 
schedule for a particular month. The calendar may be filled in on line, saved, changed, 
emailed, or printed. 

ONG DISTANCE PARENTING PLAN FORM 

This form is specially designed for parents who live more than 60 miles away from each 
other. It is a master form with check boxes and simple choices.  It offers 
opportunities throughout to help a parent write a plan that fits his or her family’s needs.   
It can be filled in on line, saved, changed, emailed or printed.  A parent may print the 
blank form and fill it in by hand. 

TTACHMENTS TO LONG DISTANCE FORM

NTRODUCTION AND INSTRUCTIONS 
This page includes safety screening questions to help a parent decide whether to use 
the basic parenting plan and guide or to use the safety-focused plan and guide.  It  
explains the three form options and when they may be used. It gives basic instructions 
for filling out the safety-focused forms. 

AFETY-FOCUSED PARENTING PLAN FORM - OPTION A (SUPERVISED) 

This parenting plan form may be used when parenting time and/or exchanges (when the 
child/ren go from one parent to the other) need to be supervised. 

AFETY FOCUSED PARENTING PLAN FORM - OPTION B (UNSUPERVISED/ 

NO OVERNIGHTS) 
This parenting plan form may be used when parenting time and/or exchanges (when the 
child/ren go from one parent to the other) do not need to be supervised, but the 
child/ren do not stay overnight with one of the parents. 

AFETY FOCUSED PARENTING PLAN FORM - OPTION C (OVERNIGHTS) 

This parenting plan form may be used when parenting time and/or exchanges do not 
need to be supervised, and the child/ren stay overnight at both parents’ homes, but 
there is a need for safety measures during exchanges (when the child/ren go from one 
parent to the other).
