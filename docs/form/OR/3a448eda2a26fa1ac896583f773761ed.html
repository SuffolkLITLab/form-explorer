About OJD Guide and File and this Guide 

OJD  Guide  and  File  is  a  free  resource  provided  by  the  Oregon  Judicial  Department  (OJD)  to 
assist  you  in  filling  out  OJD  approved  court  forms.  Be  aware  that  both  the  Oregon  Judicial 
Department  and  local  court  staff  CANNOT  PROVIDE  LEGAL  ADVICE.  Court  cases  and 
proceedings can be complex. If you have questions about what you need to do you may need to 
consult an attorney. The Oregon State Bar provides resources for learning the law and finding 
an attorney. You can access their public webpage here: https://www.osbar.org/public/. 

The purpose of this guide is to assist you in using OJD Guide and File. Topics include how to 
register,  how  to  navigate  through  an  interview,  how  to  access  help  content,  and  how  to 
complete the interview process.

How to Register for Guide and File 
It is recommended that you create an account within Guide and File to save your answers and 
forms, and to be able to electronically file (eFile) documents with the courts (where available). A 
Guide and File account will give you access to OJD Guide and File and OJD File and Serve (the 
Oregon Circuit Court’s eFiling system).  

NOTE: You do not need an account to use Guide and File and print your forms.

Completing an Interview 
After  completing  an  interview,  Guide  and  File,  will  build  your  form(s)  and  provide  you  with  a 
Next Steps document. The system will ask you to view your documents. Review the documents 
provided and ensure that the information is accurate.  

IMPORTANT: You should always download or print your documents (even if you are eFiling). It 
is important to always keep a copy of the forms you filled out.  

Always  read  the  next  step  document  carefully.  The  Next  Steps  document  may  contain  time 
sensitive information including information about deadlines for completing certain steps for your
