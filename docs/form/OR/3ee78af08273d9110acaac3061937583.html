Exhibit Instructions

 

ffective February 15, 2023 

eneral to all case types 

In all trials or evidentiary hearings of any type, parties must bring to the proceeding 
the original of all exhibits and shall provide those to the court room clerk; plus, 
sufficient number of copies of each exhibit to provide to each party or their attorney, 
and the judge. 

•  Exhibits must be marked in accordance with the provisions of UTCR 6.080.  

•  Submittal of exhibits via File and Serve or Guide and File will not be accepted.   

•  Any party bringing media exhibits, such as video and audio recordings, must also 

bring with them a device capable of presenting or displaying the media content.  This 
pertains to all media exhibits of any format, including content on cell phone, laptop, 
other portable device, and external memory chip and USB connector drives. 
Questions regarding connection to the court’s playback monitors must be resolved at 
least 48 hours prior to the scheduled appearance and may be directed to:  
BEN.Exhibits@ojd.state.or.us  

Specific to juvenile case types 

Specific to civil case types 

In addition to complying with these instructions, the format and submittal of exhibits 
in juvenile matters shall also comply with UTCR 11.110.   

In civil cases, trial exhibit lists must be delivered to the court and opposing counsel 
or party not less than two judicial days prior to the commencement of trial. (SLR 
6.051(1)) 

•  Trial exhibit lists must be delivered to the assigned judge’s Judicial Assistant: 

o  Hon. Joan Demarest – BEN.JED.Scheduling@ojd.state.or.us  
o  Hon. Matthew Donohue – bentonmjdscheduling@ojd.state.or.us  
o  Hon. Locke Williams – bentonlawscheduling@ojd.state.or.us 

 
Posted in accordance with Supplemental Local Rule 6.051(4)
