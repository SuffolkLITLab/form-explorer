Notes about Individual Tables 

A case is filed with a Circuit Court when an initiating document is filed with the court or a case is transferred from one Circuit Court to 
another Circuit Court. Cases on this report were initiated during the reporting period. 

A case is terminated in a Circuit Court when a final disposition resolving all the issues on the case is issued or the case is transferred from 
one Circuit Court to another Circuit Court. Cases on this report were terminated during the reporting period.

A case is pending in a Circuit Court when the case has been filed but not yet terminated. Pending cases may be active or inactive. A case is 
considered “active” when it is within the circuit court’s control to move it forward to final disposition. Cases on this report are both 
pending and active on the final day of the reporting  period. 

Table 4 – Cases Per Authorized Judicial Position 

This report compares the number of cases filed for the reporting period to the number of authorized judicial positions for th e same 
reporting period. 

Table 5 – Age of Pending Cases 

The age of a case is the number of days a case is “active” with the court. A case is considered active when it is within the circuit court’s 
control to move it forward to final disposition. This report counts the number of active days on each pending case from the filed date to 
the last day of the reporting period. 

Table 6 – Cases Tried Analysis 
Note: This report is not currently available for 2017. 

This report provides data on the number of trials completed during the reporting period. The report provides length and durat ion of 
trials and type of trial. The report also provides the time to trial, which is the total active time on the case from the date of filing until 
the first day of the trial.

Table 7 – Goals for Timely Disposition 

 

 

 

 

 

The Oregon Judicial Conference set the Oregon Goals for Timely Disposition based on American Bar Association guidelines as 
modified for Oregon. The reports reflect cases terminated during the reporting period within specific case categories and tim e lines. 

The percent of cases meeting each goal is cumulative. For example, the percent meeting the 98% goal includes the count of tho se 
meeting the 90% goal; the percent meeting the 100% goal includes the count of both those in the 98% and 90% goals. The percen t of 
cases beyond the goal is not cumulative. 

The “Civil-General” category includes all case types in the Civil case classification. 

The “Domestic Relations” category includes Dissolution and Other Domestic Relations case classifications. The “Domestic Re lations” 
category does not have a 98% goal. 

The “Summary Civil” category includes both the Small Claims and Landlord Tenant case classifications. Summary civil has only  a 
100% goal.
