UNIFORM ENFORCEMENT OF FOREIGN JUDGMENT S ACT ( UEFJA ) REGISTRATION A foreign judgment is a judgment or order entered by a court in another state or country. The Uniform Enforcement of Foreign Judgment s Act (UEFJA) can be used for registering any type of foreign judgment or order. If you wish to register a judgment or order under the UEFJA, you will need to file : FM summary sheet (FM -002) ; Affidavit and Request for Registration of a Foreign Order (FM -058) ; and One certified copy of the foreign judgment . The clerk will complete a Notice of Registration and mail it to the parties and the police de partment if the judgment is a Protection Order. If you want the court to do something in addition to registering the judgment, you will also need to file a motion and pay a filing fee. UNIFORM CHILD CUSTODY AND JURISDICTION ENFORCEMENT ACT ( UCCJEA ) REGISTRATION The Uniform Child Custody and Jurisdiction Enforcement Act (UCCJEA) may be used to register custody judgment or orders from another state or country. If you wish to register a judgment or order under the UCCJEA, you will need to file : FM summary sheet (FM -002) ; Affidavit and Request for Registration of a Foreign Order (FM -058) ; and One certified copy and one plain copy of the foreign judgment or order . The clerk will complete a Notice of Registration and mail it to the parties. T he Notice of Registration advises the responding party of the right to request a hearing on the registration within 20 days. If the responding party makes a timely request the clerk will schedule a hearing and will notify the parties. If no hearing is r equested within 20 days, the clerk sends a Notice of Confirmation to the parties. This means the registration process is complete. You need not do anything further unless you would like the court to take further action, in which case you will need to fi le a motion and pay a filing fee. UNIFORM CHILD CUSTODY AND JURISDICTION ENFORCEMENT ACT ( UCCJEA ) - EXPEDITED ENFORCEMENT The UCCJEA includes an expedited process for enforcing a foreign custody order. If the judge determines the petition and application meet the requirements of the UCCJEA, an ex parte hearing will be scheduled on the same day you file the petition, i f possible. Following the hearing, the judge may issue a warrant or an order directing the other party to appear at a later hearing. The order will state the date and time of the hearing. The petition, warrant (if one is issued) , and order must be served on the other party. A law enforcement agency will do this. The court clerk can assist you in communicating with law enforcement. UNIFORM INTERSTATE FAMILY SUPPORT ACT (UIFSA) The Uniform Interstate Family Support Act (UIFSA) is used to govern interstate paternity and child support proceedings. Most UIFSA matters can be processed administratively at the Maine Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS) . If the matter cannot be handled by DHHS, a UIFSA Petition or a Request for Registration of a Foreign Order, along with accompanying paperwork, must be filed with the appropriate court.
