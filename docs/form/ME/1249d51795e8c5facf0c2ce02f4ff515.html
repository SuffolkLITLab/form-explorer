INFORMATION ON TRAFFIC VIOLATION APPEALS 

➢  Please note: All appeals forms may be obtained in person at any court clerk’s office or on the 
Maine Judicial Branch (MJB) website listed above. A list of all clerk’s offices and their physical 
locations can also be found on the MJB website. 
 

1.  Notice of Appeal form. You must fill out a Notice of Appeal form (MJBVB-20) for each 
violation you are appealing. 
 
2.  Twenty-one-day deadline. You must file the completed Notice of Appeal form within 21 
calendar days of the day of the decision in your case.  

 

.  Where to File. The Notice of Appeal is filed at the Violations Bureau (address listed above) and 

must be received before the 21-day deadline. If it is received late, the appeal will be dismissed. 
Please keep this in mind when mailing. 

.  Appeal fee. There is a $175 filing fee for each violation that you are appealing. This fee is 
required at the same time the Notice of Appeal is filed. The appeal fee must be in the form of a 
check or money order made payable to: Treasurer State of Maine. 

.  If you are unable to pay the fee. If you are unable to pay the appeal fee, you can request to 
have the appeal fee waived. To make this request you must fill out Application to Proceed Without 
Payment of Fees (CV-67), together with the financial Affidavit (CV-191).  

.  Transcript.  If your trial was recorded and you want a written transcript of that recording, 
you  must also fill out a  Transcript and Audi o  Order  Form (C V - CR-JV-165).  You must  file 
the  Transcript  Order  Form  at  the  same  time  you  file  the  Notice  of  Appeal.  The  clerk's  office 
wh er e  your   t r ial   wa s   held  can  tell  you if your trial was recorded and give information 
regarding the recording that will be needed to complete the form (such as what courtroom it was 
in and what the start/finish times were.). Incomplete forms will be returned and must be 
completed to process. If you do not file a Transcript Order Form within 7 days of the date you 
file the Notice of Appeal, the appeal  will proceed  i n   t h e   L a w  C o u r t   without the transcript. 

If  you  are  unable  to  pay  for  the  cost  of  the  transcript,  you  can  request  to  have  it  made 
at  the expense of the State on the Transcript Order Form. You must also fill out and submit a 
Motion for Transcript at State Expense (CV-CR-166) to complete the request. 

or detailed information about transcripts, such as cost, payment options, and how long it will 

take to complete, please see the Transcript Order Form (CV-CR-JV-165).  

.  Law Court. Your appeal will be sent to the Supreme Court, sitting as the Law Court, when 
the Notice of Appeal, fee(s), and all other necessary documents are received. The Law Court 
clerk will then notify you that your appeal has been docketed and what happens next.
