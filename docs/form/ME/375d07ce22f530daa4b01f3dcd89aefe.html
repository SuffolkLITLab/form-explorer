MOTION TO REOPEN INSTRUCTIONS 

 
A Motion to Reopen is a request made by a defendant who has had a judgment entered 
against them on a traffic citation (ticket) and would like a judge to reopen the matter for 
further review. This request is made using the Motion to Reopen (form number MJBVB-
18). There is a required $25 filing fee (per violation) to file the Motion to Reopen. If you 
are unable to pay the filing fee you may request that a judge waive it using the 
Application to Proceed Without Payment of Fees (form CV-67) and supporting Affidavit 
(form CV-191). These forms may be found on the Maine Judicial Branch website 
(www.courts.maine.gov) or may be picked up in person at any clerk’s office. Visit the 
court website for locations of all state courts.  

o file by mail, send the filing to the following address: 

Violations Bureau 
P.O. Box 480 
Lewiston, ME 04243-0480 

hen filing a motion to reopen, be aware of the following: 

•  Either you or your attorney (if you have one) must sign the motion. 

•  The $25 filing fee (per violation) is non-refundable and cannot be applied to 

 

fines. 

• 

If the violation(s) you would like to reopen relate to a failure to produce 
evidence of insurance/registration/license and/or permit, you may submit valid 
proof when filing the Motion to Reopen. 

 
•  Any outstanding fines remain due and any license/registration suspensions 

ordered in this case remain in effect unless otherwise ordered.  

hen the Violations Bureau has received your Motion to Reopen it will be given to a 
judge to review. If it is granted, the case will be set for trial OR it may be dismissed. If it 
is denied, the judgment stands and the fine previously assessed remains due. A copy of 
the judge’s decision will be sent to you at the address provided on your motion. 

nclude your name, citation number, and current address on all correspondence sent to 
the Violations Bureau.  

f you have questions, call the Violations Bureau at 207-783-5422 or visit the Maine 
Judicial Branch website.
