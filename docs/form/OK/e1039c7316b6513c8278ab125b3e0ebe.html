HAT IS THE ORAL PROFICIENCY INTERVIEW? 

n Oral Proficiency  Interview (OPI) is an assessment of functional speaking ability in a given 
language. The test measures how well a person can use a language effectively and appropriately 
by assessing performance on a range of different language tasks. 

epending on the language being tested, the interview may be in one of two formats: the Live 
Spoken Oral Proficiency Interview (OPI) or a computer-based Oral Proficiency Interview (OPIc). 

mportant!!    You  should  pay  attention  to  the  format  which  will  be 
used for your exam!!   

PIc:  In an OPIc, the candidate is interviewed via a series of computer  generated questions. 
During the course of the interview, the candidate is guided to engage in a variety of tasks such as 
describing, narrating, and hypothesizing. The interview is recorded and takes approximately 30-
40 minutes to complete. This interview is scored by trained certified raters. 

he  OPIc  is  administered  via  a  computer,  and  the  interview  is  conducted  using  speakers  or 

headphones and a microphone for the candidate to hear and respond.   
 

Languages Available for the OPIc:  The OPIc is available in the following languages:  
Arabic, Mandarin Chinese, French, German, *Italian, Japanese, Korean, Pashto, Persian 
Farsi, European Portuguese, *Brazilian Portuguese, Russian and Spanish 
(*Limitations apply) 

f the OPIc is not available in the language being tested, the live OPI exam will be used.  However, 
the  Board  of  Examiners  for  Certified  Courtroom  Interpreters  may  determine,  on  per-language 
basis, that a written translation exam will be used in lieu of the oral proficiency interview.   

ive OPI:  In a live OPI, the candidate is interviewed over the phone by a tester who guides the 
conversation  to  explore  the  abilities  and  limits  of  the  individual’s  oral  target  language  skills. 
During the course of the interview, the candidate is guided to engage in a variety of tasks such as 
describing,  narrating,  and  hypothesizing.  The  interview  takes  approximately  30  minutes  to 
complete and is recorded and scored by the original interviewer as well as a second rater.

SCHEDULING THE ORAL PROFICIENCY INTERVIEW 

f you pass the NCSC written exam, you are eligible to take the Oral Proficiency Interview.  You 
must  contact  the  AOC  to  schedule  your  exam.    Usually,  several  dates  and  time  slots  will  be 
available.  Exams are scheduled on a first-come, first-served basis. 

ccasionally, a written translation exam will be used for a designated language in lieu of the Oral 
Proficiency Interview.  You will be advised at the time of scheduling if your exam will be a written 
translation exam. 

AY OF THE EXAM  

ll exams will be administered at the Administrative Office of the Courts in Oklahoma City, unless 
an alternative location has been announced.  On the day of your exam, you must check in at your 
designated exam time.  Candidates are encouraged to arrive early, and to allow plenty of time to 
park and pass through building security.  AOC staff will check you in, and will proctor the exam.  
AOC  staff  will  be  present  during  the  exam  and  will  monitor  candidates  throughout  the  exam 
process.

OW TO PREPARE 

he best preparation for an Oral Proficiency Interview is to have conversations on a wide variety 
of topics. Speaking about oneself, current events, as well as abstract topics that require elaboration 
on opinions or descriptive narrations are usually areas covered in an OPI. Practice elaborating on 
opinions, or supporting arguments for or against different topics.  

peak in your non-English language as much as possible, preferably with native speakers of that 
language who can engage you in conversation and correct any mistakes you make. Analyze any 
mistakes  you  do make  and try to identify  patterns (i.e., number/gender  agreement  errors, tense 
errors, etc.) and then eliminate them. When speaking, force yourself to elaborate. Reading widely 
and listening to broadcasts in  your working language that  are high-quality linguistically is also 
helpful. 

OTE:  The  OPI for Oklahoma Registered  Interpreters is  NOT the  same  as  the  oral  exam for 
Certified Courtroom Interpreters.  You must first become Registered before you are  eligible to 
take the oral exam for full certification.
