QUALIFICATIONS:  Candidates must currently be enrolled as a Registered Courtroom Interpreter in Oklahoma in 
accordance with the rules. 

VERVIEW OF THE ORAL EXAM:  Candidates are encouraged to review the Court Interpreter Oral Examination 
Overview located in the three-ring binder  you received at the  Oklahoma Interpreter Orientation Training Program.  
This Overview contains important information regarding the oral examination.  You may also find a link to the Court 
Interpreter  Oral  Examination  Overview  and  additional  resources  on  the  Oklahoma  Supreme  Court’s  website, 
www.oscn.net.  Look under Programs for the “Certified Courtroom Interpreters” section. 
    
SCHEDULING:  The Spring, 2022 exams will be offered during two days – March 23 & 24, 2022.  On the application 
form, candidates are asked to select a preferred date.  Exams will be scheduled on a first-come, first-served basis.  
If the requested date is already full, we will attempt to contact you to discuss alternate dates.  However, if we cannot 
reach you, this office will select an alternate test date (if one is available).    

ONFIRMATION:    Once  you  have  successfully  registered,  you  will  receive  a  confirmation  from  this  office  which 
includes the date and the assigned time for your oral examination.  You should plan to arrive at least 20 minutes 
before your assigned exam time for registration.  Candidates who arrive late will not be allowed to take the exam. 

EGISTRATION DEADLINE:  The deadline for registering and paying the exam fee is March 3, 2022.

General Information – Oral Certification March, 2022 
 

OKLAHOMA COURT RULES 

ULES:  The  Oklahoma  Courtroom  Interpreter  program  is  governed  by  Supreme  Court  Rules  titled  “The  Rules  of  the 
State Board of Examiners of Certified Courtroom Interpreters.” These are located in the Oklahoma Statutes at Title 20, 
Chapter 23, Appendix II. Certified Courtroom Interpreters are addressed in Rules 2, 3, and 11: 

ule 2. Levels of Courtroom Interpreter Credentials.   Paragraph (a) discusses certified courtroom interpreters: 

a)  A  “Certified  Courtroom  Interpreter”  (also  referred  to  herein  as  a  “Certified  Interpreter”)  is  a  foreign  language 
interpreter  who  has  achieved  certification  –  the  highest  credential  for  courtroom  interpreting  in  Oklahoma  – 
pursuant to the requirements of these Rules, and who holds a current certification status with the Board. 

)  Certified Interpreters are identified on the Oklahoma Courtroom Interpreter registry which is maintained by 

the Administrative Office of the Courts and which may be posted on the Supreme Court’s website. 
 

2)  An interpreter certified pursuant to these is qualified to act as a courtroom interpreter in both translation of 

written text and interpretation of spoken words in the language certified. 

ule 3. General Requirements for Eligibility. 

a)  Every candidate who seeks to attain the status of Registered or Certified Courtroom Interpreter shall:

)  Prove to the satisfaction of the Board that he/she is: 

a)  of legal age;

2)  Submit to the Board properly completed application forms, accompanied by such evidence, statements or

b)  Academic  dishonesty  during  the  examination  process  will  result  in  the  applicant's  disqualification,  and  the 
applicant  may  not  take  the  examination  again  until  five  (5)  years  have  elapsed  from  the  date  of  the 
examination at which the applicant is disqualified. 

Rule 11. Certified Courtroom Interpreter Requirements and Oral Interpreter Examination 

a)  To become a Certified Courtroom Interpreter in a spoken language, the candidate must: 

1)  Be currently enrolled as a Registered Courtroom Interpreter in Oklahoma in accordance with these Rules;  

2)  Pass the NCSC Court Interpreter Oral Examination in the language being certified. 

b)  The NCSC Court Interpreter Oral Examination prescribed in the paragraph above shall be conducted at least 
once  per  calendar  year  and  shall  consist  of  the  following  three  sections:  Simultaneous  Interpreting, 
Consecutive  Interpreting,  and  Sight  Translation  of  Documents.  The  Sight  Translation  section  of  the  exam 
consists  of  two  parts  --  sight  translation  of  a  document  written  in  English  interpreted  orally  into  the  non-
English language and sight translation of a document written in the non-English language interpreted into oral 
English.

percent (70%) or better in each of the three sections of the examination. The scores of Part I and Part II of 
the Sight Translation section are combined for one overall score for that section. 

2)  The oral examination shall be administered and rated in accordance with the test administration and rating

3)  The Board shall charge the applicant a fee in an amount approved by the Supreme Court for each section 

4)  A candidate must initially take all three sections of the oral exam in the same test sitting, and may retain 
credit for passing score(s) on each section of the exam for twenty-four (24) months, unless an exception is 
granted by the Board.  During the 24-month period, the candidate must retest at least once per year, and 
may take only the exam section(s) the candidate has not passed. 

5)  If more than one version of the NCSC oral examination for the same language is available, an applicant 
who  fails  to  pass  the  oral  examination  must  wait  six  (6)  months  to  re-test,  and  must  take  a  different 
version of the examination. An applicant may not take the same version of the oral examination more than 
once in a twelve (12) month period. 

6)  An applicant who has passed the NCSC oral examination in another state within the past twenty-four (24) 
months may apply to the Board for recognition of the score. The applicant shall prove to the satisfaction 
of the Board that the passing score is substantially comparable to that required by this Rule. 

c)  For languages in which the NCSC oral exam is unavailable, the Board may utilize an abbreviated NCSC oral 
examination, if one is available. If no abbreviated NCSC oral examination is available, the Board may, at its 
discretion, recognize other oral proficiency examinations or interviews on a per-language basis.
