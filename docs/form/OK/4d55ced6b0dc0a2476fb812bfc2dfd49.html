DIRECTIONS FOR APPLYING FOR CERTIFICATION BY RECIPROCITY 

nder the Oklahoma Statutes, Title 20, Section 1510, a person holding a license or certificate from another state 
which is deemed by the State Board of Examiners of Certified Shorthand Reporters to be equivalent to that of 
an Oklahoma certified shorthand reporter, or holding current national certification as a shorthand reporter, or 
holding both an equivalent license from another state and a national certification, may be enrolled without taking 
the required Oklahoma skills examinations.  Applicants for admission by reciprocity must satisfy the Board that 
their  credentials  are  in  proper  order,  and,  prior  to  certification,  must  pass  the  Oklahoma  Written  Knowledge 
portion of the examination.  

o  be  deemed  equivalent  to  an  Oklahoma  certificate,  the  certification  test  must  demonstrate  proficiency  in 
reporting  testimony  and  proceedings  at  a  speed  of  at  least  two  hundred  (200)  words  per  minute  in  taking  a 
question-and-answer  type  dictation  and  at  a  speed  of  at  least  one  hundred  eighty  (180)  words  per minute  in 
taking literary materials.  The Oklahoma examination includes five (5) minutes of each type of dictation, allows 
one (1) hour to transcribe each part, and requires at least ninety-five percent (95%) accuracy. 

pplicants should do the following:   

 

 

1.  Call the Administrative Office of the Courts at (405) 556-9300.  Inform that office of your intent to request 
Oklahoma CSR certification by reciprocity.  Request that a copy of the link for the Handbook for Oklahoma 
Official Court Reporters be emailed to you. 
 

2.  Forward to the Administrative Office of the Courts, the following items:  

a.  A properly completed “Application for Admission by Reciprocity” 
b.  Evidence from the National Court Reporters Association of holding a current national certification 
as a shorthand reporter, OR evidence of equivalent certification from another state.  Include a 
copy of your certificate, and evidence that your certificate is current.  (Note:  You may be asked 
to provide additional information, such as a letter from an official under whose auspices the test 
was  conducted,  stating  the  dictation  speed,  type  of  examination,  duration,  time  allowed  for 
transcription, required accuracy, and method of testing.) 

c.  A check or money order payable to Clerk of the Supreme Court in the sum of $150.00. 

3.  If your current name is different from that listed on your submitted certificate, you must provide legal proof 

of the name change, such as a marriage certificate or divorce decree.   
 

4.  Make arrangements with a Board Member to take the Oklahoma Written Knowledge examination.
