RDERED that: 

 

1. 

The Court’s Order establishing the Supreme Court Advisory Committee issued in 
Misc.  Docket  No.  18-9032  (March  1,  2018)  and  supplemented  in  Misc.  Docket  No.  19-9074 
(August 23, 2019) is vacated, and this Order is substituted. 
 

The Supreme Court Advisory Committee, first created in 1940 and reconstituted at various 
times since then, assists the Supreme Court in the continuing study, review, and development of 
rules and procedures for the courts of Texas, taking into consideration the rules and procedures of 
other courts in the United States and proposals for change from whatever source received. The 
Committee drafts rules as directed by the Court; solicits, summarizes, and reports to the Court the 
views of the bar and the public on court rules and procedures; and makes recommendations for 
change. The Court is not bound by the Committee’s recommendations. 
 

Committee meetings are held after public notice and are open to the public. A record is 
made of all Committee proceedings. Committee expenses are paid from funds appropriated by the 
Legislature or the State Bar of Texas. 
 

Members of the Committee are appointed by the Supreme Court, which shall from time to 
time determine their number, qualifications, and terms of service. A Chair appointed by the Court 
to serve at its pleasure calls meetings with the approval of the Court, prepares an agenda in advance 
of  each  meeting,  and  presides  over  the  meetings.  Proposals,  drafts,  and  other  materials  to  be 
discussed are circulated before meetings.
