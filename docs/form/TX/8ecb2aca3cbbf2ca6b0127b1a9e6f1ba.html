AMENDED ORDER GRANTING DEFENDANT'S MOTION TO DECLARE 
PLAINTIFF FREDERICK C. FERMIN A VEXATIOUS LITIGANT 

On March 13, 2015, came to be heard Defendant Financial Freedom Senior Funding 

Corporation's Motion to Declare Plaintiff Frederick C. Fermin a Vexatious Litigant. The Court, 

having considered the motion, the pleadings on file, and argument of counsel, finds that the 

motion is meritorious and should be granted. It is therefore, 

ORDERED, ADJUDGED AND DECREED that Defendant's Motion to Declare Plaintiff 

a Vexatious Litigant is hereby GRANTED. 

IT IS FURTHER DECLARED that Plaintiff Frederick C. Fermin is a vexatious litigant. 

IT IS FURTHER ORDERED, ADJUDGED AND DECREED that Frederick C. Fermin 

is prohibited from filing as a pro se litigant any litigation in the State of Texas without the 

permission of a local administrative judge who first reviews said litigation, pursuant to section 

11.102 of the Texas Civil Practice and Remedies Code, and determines that the litigation has 

merit and has not been filed for the purpose of harassment.
