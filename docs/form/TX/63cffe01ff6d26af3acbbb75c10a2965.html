ORDERED that: 

 

In accordance with the Act of May 13, 2015, 84th Leg., R.S., ch. 74 (S.B. 888) 
(codified at TEX. FAM. CODE § 56.01(h-1)), the Court issued Misc. Dkt. No. 15-
9156, effective September 1, 2015, to govern actions under the Juvenile Justice 
Code, Title 3 of the Family Code, pending the adoption of rules. The Court now 
invites public comment on proposed new Texas Rule of Civil Procedure 306b 
and proposed amendments to Texas Rules of Appellate Procedure 25.1, 28.4, 
and 32.1 and Texas Rule of Judicial Administration 6.2. 
 
In accordance with the Act of May 25, 2019, 86th Leg., R.S., ch. 844 (H.B. 2737) 
(codified at TEX. GOV’T CODE § 22.0135(b)), the Court invites public comment 
on proposed new Texas Rule of Judicial Administration 17. 
 
The amendments to Texas Rules of Appellate Procedure 25.1, 28.4, and 32.1 
and  Texas  Rule  of  Judicial  Administration  6.2  are  demonstrated  in  redline 
form.  New  Texas  Rule  of  Civil  Procedure  306b  and  Texas  Rule  of  Judicial 
Administration 17 are demonstrated in clean form. 
 
Comments  regarding  the  proposed  new  and  amended  rules  should  be 
submitted in writing to rulescomments@txcourts.gov by May 1, 2023.  
 
The Court will issue an order finalizing the rules after the close of the comment 
period. The Court may change the rules in response to public comments. The 
Court expects the rules to take effect on June 1, 2023. The order finalizing the 
rules will supersede Misc. Dkt. No. 15-9156.

RULE 306b. ADVICE OF RIGHT TO APPEAL IN ORDERS CERTIFYING A 
CHILD TO STAND TRIAL AS AN ADULT (Clean Form)  
  
When a juvenile court issues an order under Family Code section 54.02 certifying a 
child  to  stand  trial  as  an  adult,  the  court  must  inform  the  child  and  the  child’s 
attorney, orally on the record and in writing in the certification order, that:  
  

the child may immediately appeal the certification order under Family 
Code section 56.01; and  

the appeal is accelerated under Texas Rule of Appellate Procedure 28.1.
