NOTICE TO ALL PARTIES 

If you want to file a response to this petition, you must do so within 20 days after you 
receive it.  If you are a parent or Indian Custodian, you can use form CN-345, Notice of 
Parent’s/Indian Custodian’s Position on Proposed Transfer of Case, available online at 
https://public.courts.alaska.gov/web/forms/docs/cn-345.pdf or from the state court clerk. 

If either parent opposes the transfer to tribal court, the petition will not be granted unless 
the parental rights of the parent have already been terminated by a court with the authority 
to do so.

  25 U.S.C. § 1911(b).

 

If the tribal court declines jurisdiction at any time before an order granting the petition to 
transfer is signed, the petition will not be granted. 

If any party in the state court case shows good cause for why the case should not be 
transferred, the petition will not be granted.

 

If jurisdiction is transferred to tribal court, and the tribal court exercises jurisdiction, any 
state court lawyer or guardian ad litem (GAL) appointments will terminate (end). 

To accept or decline jurisdiction, the tribal court may file form CN-340, Order Regarding 
Jurisdiction over Indian Child Welfare Case, with the state court.  The form is available at 
https://public.courts.alaska.gov/web/forms/docs/cn-340.pdf or from the state court clerk. 

State court forms may be found at www.courts.alaska.gov.
