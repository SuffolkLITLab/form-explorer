SMALL CLAIMS INFORMATION SHEET 

What is a Small Claims Case? 

A small claims case is a simplified type of court case for a person to try to recover money or personal 
property worth $10,000 or less.  If your claim is over $10,000, you can still use small claims court, but 
you must give up the right to collect any amount over $10,000.  You may, however, recover interest on 
any money you are awarded and court costs over the $10,000 maximum. 
You do not need a lawyer in a small claims case, although you may have one if you wish.  Small claims 
court can only be used if all parties agree to use this simplified procedure. 
Small claims procedure cannot be used for: 

 disagreements about title to real property (land or buildings) 
 actions to recover possession of real property (land or buildings) 
 evictions 
 claims against the State of Alaska or the United States government 
 injunctive relief (a court order requiring a person to do or not to do a specified act) 
 actions to foreclose or enforce statutory, common law, or possessory liens 

You can find more information about small claims at https://courts.alaska.gov/shc/sc/index.htm  
The Alaska Small Claims Handbook and all small claims forms are also available in paper copy from any 
local court clerk.
