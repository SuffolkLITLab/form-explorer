You are not required to attend this hearing unless you are called as a witness. 

urpose of Hearing   

At the hearing, the court will decide whether the respondent needs a conservator because (1) 
the respondent is unable to manage the respondent’s own money and property effectively, and 
(2)  the  respondent  has  money  or  property  that  will  be  wasted  or  lost  unless  proper 
management is provided.  A conservator may also be appointed if the respondent needs help in 
order  to  financially  support  the  respondent’s  self,  or  others  the  respondent  has  a  financial 
obligation to support (for example, minor children). 

ossible Consequences  

If  the  court  decides  to  appoint  a  conservator,  the  conservator  will  have  control  of  the 
respondent’s  money  and  property.    Alternatively,  the  court  may  decide  to  appoint  only  a 
"special  conservator"  who  will  have  more  limited  powers.    Or,  the  court  may  decide  not  to 
appoint a conservator at all or to order a different form of protection.
