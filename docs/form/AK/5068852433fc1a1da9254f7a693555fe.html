AFTER SENTENCING INSTRUCTIONS 

Criminal Rule 32.6 (c) 

FINES, RESTITUTION, SURCHARGES, COST OF IMPRISONMENT 

Fines are a financial punishment that you must pay to the state or municipality. Restitution is money 
the court orders you to pay for the loss or injury to the victim of your crime.  Surcharges and costs of 
imprisonment are other costs you are required by law to pay.   

8.  HOW AND WHERE TO PAY  

Find payment instructions at www.courts.alaska.gov/trialcourts/payments.htm, or contact your local  
court clerk for more information about payment options (in person, by mail, or online).  
  Where: 825 West 4th Avenue in Anchorage (Nesbett Courthouse)  
  When: 8:00 a.m.-4:30 p.m. Monday-Thursday. 8:00 a.m.-12:00 p.m. Friday 
  Phone: 264-0514 

If you do not pay by the due date, a writ of execution may be used to take money from your PFD, bank 
accounts, and wages to pay the fine, restitution, surcharges, and cost of imprisonment.  If you do not pay 
your restitution, you may also be arrested and the suspended part of your jail sentence and/or fine may be 
imposed.  For more information, read the Restitution Information Handout, form CR-786 ANCH. 

9. 

CREDIT TO FINE FOR PROGRAM FEES 
If your judgment has a checkmark by “Fees paid to court-ordered programs (except CWS) will be 
deducted from this fine if proof of payment is presented to the court clerk by the fine due date,” 
then your payments for court-ordered programs can be credited toward your fine.  You will only 
receive credit if you bring written proof of payment of those fees to the court by the 
fine due date listed on your judgment.  

You may be charged with an infraction or a new crime if you drive while your driver’s license is 
revoked, suspended, limited, or has not been reinstated.  If ordered not to consume alcohol, you 
are also restricted from buying alcohol, and any license/state ID issued to you under AS 18.65.310 
must list this restriction.  If you have not surrendered your license/state ID yet, you will need to 
surrender it to the DMV.  For more information about license actions and reinstatement, go to the 
DMV website at www.doa.alaska.gov/dmv/.  

Where: 3901 Old Seward Hwy, Suite 101 (Anchorage DMV main office) 
When: 9:00 a.m.-5:00 p.m. Monday-Friday 
Phone: 269-5551

12.  IGNITION INTERLOCK DEVICE (IID)   

You must pay all costs associated with the IID, including the cost of installation, repair, and 
monitoring.  To receive credit for the costs of the IID against the fine imposed in your 
case you must submit proof of payment to the clerk by the fine due date stated in your 
judgment.  After that date, the fine will be sent to collections and credit will not be given.  For 
more information, read the Ignition Interlock Device Information Sheet, form CR-483.
