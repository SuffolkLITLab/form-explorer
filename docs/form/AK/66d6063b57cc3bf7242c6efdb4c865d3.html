 am fully advised of the following: 

Under the Alaska Constitution, I have a right to have the above charge presented to a 
Grand Jury for review. 

A Grand Jury consists of 12 to 18 citizens who hear evidence presented by the District 
Attorney through witnesses. 

During  the  presentation  of  a  case  before  the  Grand  Jury,  only  the  District  Attorney,  a 
clerk, and the witness are present. 

The  Grand  Jury  can  return  an  indictment  against  me  only  upon  the  concurrence  of  a 
majority  of  the  total  number  of  jurors  and  when  all  the  evidence  taken  together,  if 
unexplained or uncontradicted, would warrant a conviction of me at trial. 

If  the  Grand  Jury  declines  to  indict  me  after  reviewing  the  evidence,  the  effect  is  to 
dismiss the charge which was before them; and that charge may not be again submitted 
to the Grand Jury without a court order. 

The  effect  of  an  indictment  being  returned  by  the  Grand  Jury  is  to  place  the  charges 
against me before the Superior Court. 

The effect of a Waiver of Indictment by me will be to allow the charges against me to be 
placed before the Superior Court without the Grand Jury having reviewed the evidence 
in my case.
