“Mental  illness”  means  an  organic,  mental,  or  emotional  impairment  that  has  substantial  adverse  effects  on  a  person’s 
ability  to  exercise  conscious  control  of  their  actions  or  ability  to  perceive  reality  or  to  reason  or  understand;  intellectual 
disability, developmental disability, epilepsy, drug addiction, and alcoholism do not per se constitute mental illness, although 
persons suffering from these conditions may also be suffering from mental illness.  AS 47.30.915.

  Likely to cause serious harm to the respondent’s self, because:
 
 
 
  Likely to cause serious harm to others because: 
 
 
 

 

“Likely to cause serious harm" means a person who (A) poses a substantial risk of bodily harm to that person's self, as 
manifested by recent behavior causing, attempting, or threatening that harm;  (B) poses a substantial risk of harm to others 
as manifested by recent behavior causing, attempting, or threatening harm, and is likely in the near future to cause physical 
injury, physical abuse, or substantial property damage to another person; or (C) manifests a current intent to carry out plans
