What is the Fairbanks Veterans 
Court? 
The Fairbanks Veterans Court is a 
specialized court designed to facilitate the 
rehabilitation of  eligible veterans who are 
charged with criminal offenses.  The Court 
provides judicial monitoring coupled with 
alternative sentencing plea agreements to 
help eligible veterans succeed with their own 
rehabilitation and return to a productive and 
law-abiding lifestyle, thereby reducing crime 
and its costs to society. 

What are the objectives of the 
Fairbanks Veterans Court? 
•  Promote public safety. 
•  Reduce incarceration of  offenders who 
are veterans and promote their well-
being in the community. 

•  Reduce repeated contacts with the 
criminal justice system among our 
nation’s veterans. 

•  Facilitate access to Veterans 

Administration (VA) resources to aid  the 
veteran with addressing problems that 
led to criminal justice contact. 
•  Promote treatment adherence for 

the veteran through ongoing judicial 
monitoring.

How do I apply for the 
Fairbanks Veterans Court? 
Admission into the FVC is not automatic, but 
is based on state prosecutor approval 
and required assessments. 

A veteran interested in participating in 
the FVC should have the veteran’s 
attorney contact the FVC Project 
Coordinator to request an application.  
Interested veterans may observe court 
proceedings with no commitment to 
participate.   

How does the FVC work?  
The FVC is a program requiring 
commitment, strength, honesty, and 
persistence.  Veterans are expected to:  
•  Attend treatment and other services as 

•  Attend recovery/support groups if 

recommended 

necessary   

•  Appear regularly before the FVC judge 

for status hearings  

•  Complete 16-32 hours of work, school, 

or volunteer work per week 

•  Undergo regular alcohol and other 

drug testing  
•  Maintain sobriety 
•  Maintain safe and sober housing  
•  Attend meetings with their PO 
•  Complete their VA case plan
