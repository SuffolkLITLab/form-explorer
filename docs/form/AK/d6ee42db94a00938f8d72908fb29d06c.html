[Use this form if you want to extend an existing domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking

When to file your request: File this form within 30 days before or within 60 days after your 
current long-term order expires.  If more than 60 days have passed since your order expired, 
you should file a new petition for protective order instead of using this form.   

hat happens after you file: The court will set a hearing with at least 10 daysâ€™ notice to 
both parties and will decide whether an extension is necessary to protect you.  This request, 
by itself, does not extend the protective order.  All parties must follow the existing 
protective order while it is in effect until a judge extends it in writing.  If your 
current order expires before the hearing, there will not be a protective order in 
place until the court extends it at the hearing or until you request a new order based 
on a new domestic violence incident.  If you fail to appear for a hearing about this request, 
the judge may deny your request.  Learn more about resources and safety planning at 
http://courts.alaska.gov/shc/shcdv.htm.
