EMERGENCY ADMINISTRATIVE ORDER 

AND  NOW,  this  21st  day  of  May,  2020,  pursuant  to  this  Court’s 

Declaration  of  Judicial  Emergency  dated  March  16,  2020  effective  through  and 

including May 31, 2020, and in accordance with the Administrative Orders issued 

by  the  Pennsylvania  Supreme  Court  on  March  18,  2020,  at  Nos.  531  and  532 

J.A.D.  (“Supreme  Court  Order”)  that  generally  closed  to  the  public  all  court 

facilities  throughout  the  Commonwealth  through  at  least  May  1,  2020  and  Order 

dated  April  28,  2020  generally  opening    Pennsylvania  courts  unless  otherwise 

provided by a local emergency order and; this Court’s Administrative Order dated 

March  17,  2020,  at  No.  65  A.D.  (“Monroe  Administrative  Order”)  and  subsequent 

Orders,  that  limit  access  to  judicial  facilities  in  the  43rd  Judicial  District  and  Court 

proceedings  through  May  31st  and;  after  consideration  of  the  recommendations 

and  guidelines  issued  by  the  CDC,  the  Pennsylvania  Department  of  Health,  and 

numerous  local  and  regional  medical  experts  consulted  by  this  Court  and;  in 

keeping  with  the  spirit  of  the  Governor’s  and  Secretary  of  Health’s  Orders;  in 

furtherance  of  the  mandate  of  Pennsylvania  Rule  of  Judicial  Administration 

1952(B)(2)  that  the  President  Judge  “take  necessary  action  to  provide  for  the 

…safety of court personnel, court users and the public…”, and following a careful 

balancing  of  the  public’s  right  of  access  to  courts  against  health,  safety,  and 

welfare  concerns  arising  from  the  COVID-19  pandemic  that  necessitated  the 

    I

   

     

Emergency Orders, we hereby AUTHORIZE and ORDER the following pursuant to 

Pa.R.J.A. No. 1952(B)(2): 

All  DUI  case  files,  a  list  of  which  is  attached  hereto,  originally 

scheduled  for  preliminary  hearing  from  March  19th  through  May  31st  in  all 

Magisterial  District  Courts  within  the  43rd  Judicial  District  shall  be  electronically 

transferred  to  Magisterial  District  Court  43-3-02  located  at  1211  N  5th  Street, 

Stroudsburg,  Pa.  for  case  processing  and  the  rescheduling  of  the  preliminary 

hearing,  pursuant  to  this  Court’s  authority  delineated  above  and  Pennsylvania 

Rule of Judicial Administration 605(B)(6). 

This Order shall remain in effect until further order of the President 

Judge of this Court or the Pennsylvania Supreme Court. 

BY THE COURT, 

 

 

 

 

MARGHERITA PATTI-WORTHINGTON, P.J.
