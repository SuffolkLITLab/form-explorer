IN THE SUPREME COURT OF PENNSYLVANIA

Filed 06/25/2021 Supreme Court Middle District 

IN RE:  49th JUDICIAL DISTRICT 
JUDICIAL EMERGENCY ORDER 

39-MM-2020 

REQUEST FOR EMERGENCY JUDICIAL ORDER 

1. 

Pamela A. Ruest, President Judge of the 49th Judicial District, hereby requests the 

following relief by Order of the Supreme Court: 

a.  Authorize the president judge to declare a judicial emergency in this judicial district 

through August 31, 2021 to take the actions set forth in  paragraph 1 b as authorized by Pa.R.J.A. 

No. 1952(8)(2). 

as follows: 

b.  Suspend or modify statewide procedural or administrative rules in this judicial district 

1.  Authorize the temporary relocation of jury selection from the County seat of 

Bellefonte, Pennsylvania to the Penn Stater Conference Center and Hotel in 

State College, Pennsylvania for August 2, 3 and 4, 2021. 

2.  Authorize the expanded use of advanced communication technology (ACT) 

to conduct court proceedings for all criminal matters, including preliminary 

hearings, waiver hearings, sentencings and probation/parole violation 

hearings. 

3.  Authorize the Prothonotary/Clerk of Courts to continue to accept electronic 

filings while a local rule is being promulgated. 

2. 

The circumstances necessitating this request for an emergency judicial order are as 

follows: 

a.  As to jury selection, a contract has been signed for use of the Penn Stater for 

August 2,  3 and 4, 2021 and jurors have already been summoned.  This locale will further allow 

the safe selection of jurors. 

b.  As to continuing to use ACT, Centre County has two State Correctional Institutions 

and using ACT is far more cost effective and reduces security risks. 

c.  As to electronic filing,  it  is beneficial to all  litigants and attorneys, and more cost 

effective. 

3. 

To the extent possible and practical under the circumstances, notice of this request for 

an emergency judicial order will be: 

a.  Posted in the courthouse or other judicial office 

b.  Posted on the court's or county's website 

c.  Posted on the county bar association's website 

d.  Submitted to the Administrative Office of Pennsylvania Courts for publication 

pursuant to Rule of Judicial Administration No. 1952(C)(5) 

4. 

Interested parties are advised that objections to any emergency judicial order issued by 

the Supreme Court should be transmitted to the Supreme Court Prothonotary.
