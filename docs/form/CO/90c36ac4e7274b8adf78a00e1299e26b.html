Step 6: Reply Brief 

1.  Purpose: 

This is your opportunity as the Appellant to counter the 

arguments that the Appellee made in the Answer Brief. 

You may not raise new issues in the Reply Brief. 

2.  Optional 

Filing a Reply Brief is optional — you are not required to file one 

if you believe it is not necessary to respond to the arguments in 

the Answer Brief. 

3.  Deadline: 

Due within 21 days after the Answer Brief. 

You may not file one if no Answer Brief is filed. 

4.  Formatting:  Colorado Appellate Rule (C.A.R.) 32 format includes: 

•  14-point font size,  

•  double line spacing,  

•  Bookman, Garamond, or Times New Roman font, and 

•  printing on only one side of the page.

You may use JDF 1917 - Reply Brief Outline.

•  Fill in the parties’ names in the order they appeared in the 

District Court. 

•  The Appellant is the person who filed the appeal. 

•  The Appellee is the person responding to the appeal.

•  State how many words are in your Reply Brief.
