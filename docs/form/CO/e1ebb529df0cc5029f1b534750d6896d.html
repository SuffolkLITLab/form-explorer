  Send the Petition to: 

1)  Prosecuting Attorney who obtained the conviction. 

2)  Prosecuting Attorney in the jurisdiction you’re filing (if different), and 

3)  Each law enforcement agency that you are required to register as a sex offender. 

  Attach the return receipts/certified mailer for each entity you sent the Petition. 

  File the Certificate within 21 days of the Petition. 

Step 2:  File Documents with the Court. 

  File the Petition, Notice of Hearing, and Order with the Court and pay any filing fees. 

  Within 21-days of filing the Petition, submit the Certificate of Mailing and attach all 

return receipts. 

  Within 21-days of filing the Petition, submit any documents supporting your eligibility 

to deregister as a sex offender.  Include records showing you completed any Court 

ordered treatment.  Be sure to send a copy to the Prosecuting Attorney. 

Step 3:  Next Steps and Hearing. 

  The Prosecuting Attorney has 63 days to object to the Petition. 

  If no objection is submitted, the Court may grant the Petition without a hearing. 

  If an objection is submitted, be prepared to present documentation or testimony at 

the hearing to support your request. 

  The Order granting or denying your request may be signed at the end of the hearing. 

Step 4:  After the Hearing. 

  If granted, send a copy of the order to each law enforcement agency and to the 

Colorado Bureau of Investigation at 690 Kipling Street, Suite 315, Lakewood, CO
