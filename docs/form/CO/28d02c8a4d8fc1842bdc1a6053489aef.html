Step 1: Notice of Appeal 

1.  Purpose: This document gives notice that you are appealing. It starts your case with the 

 

Colorado Court of Appeals.  
 

2.  Deadline: The Notice of Appeal is generally due 49 days after: 

•  Sentencing. Or, 
•  An Order denying a Motion for Postconviction Relief.  
•  A Motion to Reconsider the Sentence does not delay the 49 days. 

.  No New Facts: The Court of Appeals can consider only the facts presented in the 

District Court. No other evidence will be accepted or considered. 

.  Filing Fee: There is a one-time filing fee of $223. Pay that fee to the Court of Appeals 

with your first filing. To waive that fee:  

•  As an Inmate: File an Inmate Motion to Waive the Filing Fee - JDF 201 and Proposed 
Order - JDF 202 with the District Court. The District Court will complete the 
findings section of JDF 201. Submit this to the Court of Appeals. 

 

•  Not an Inmate: File a Motion to Waive the Filing Fee - JDF 205 and Proposed Order - 
JDF 206 with the District Court. The District Court will complete the findings 
section of JDF 206. Submit this to the Court of Appeals. 

.  The Form: use JDF 672.
