JDF 1264 

 

Steps to a 
Civil Union Divorce (no kids) 

 
Forms available at www.courts.state.co.us/Forms/family 

❑ 1.  Decide How to File. 

❑ 2.  File the Paperwork 

File with your partner by signing the Petition together (jointly) or by yourself. 

File where you or your partner reside.  Or, file in the county that issued the civil union. 

One of you must must reside in Colorado for at least 91 days. 

Forms: 

 Case Information Sheet (JDF 1000) and  Petition (JDF 1250). 

If filing by yourself also file  Summons (JDF 1251). 

Pay or waive the filing fee. 

❑ 3.  Case Management Order 

The Court will send a Case Management Order (CMO).  That order may include a date for an 

Initial Status Conference.  You may also get special instructions for your case. 

If Filing Jointly – Skip to Step 6 

❑ 4.  Formal Notification 

You must formally notify your partner of the case by a process called personal service.  This is 

done by a disinterested party (at least 18 years old) who formally delivers copies of the Step 2 

starting paperwork plus any Step 3 court documents. 

❑ 5.  Provide Proof of Service 

Provide the proof of service to the court. 

There is a mandatory 91-day waiting period before the divorce can be granted.

❑ 6.  Complete Forms 

 Certificate of Compliance (JDF 1104) (Each partner must do their own.) 
 Sworn Financial Statement (JDF 1111) (Both parties must complete their own.) 
 Property and Financial Agreement (JDF 1115) 
 Affidavit for Decree without Appearance of Parties (Civil Union) (1258) 
 Decree (JDF 1257) (Complete Caption Only) 
 Pretrial Statement (JDF 1129).  Only complete if disagreement on some or all issues. 

❑ 7.  Initial Status Conference 

The Initial Status Conference is an opportunity to discuss the status of your case and to 

exchange forms ( and ) and other documents identified in the CMO. 

If you have complete agreement on  – Skip to Step 10 

❑ 8.  Mediation 

❑ 9.  Contested Hearing 

❑ 10. Decree 

You and your partner will likely be required to participate in mediation before the Hearing.  This is 

an opportunity to resolve any issues of the divorce. 

You will need to provide proof of the mediation (JDF 1337) to the Court. 

Be prepared to present evidence and testimony regarding what you are requesting.  Complete 

and file with the Court the  Pretrial Statement (JDF 1129).  This form must be filed with the 

Court and provided to the other party 7 days prior to the hearing. 

If all documents are complete, your  Decree may be entered on or after the 92nd day or at the 

conclusion of your Contested Hearing.
