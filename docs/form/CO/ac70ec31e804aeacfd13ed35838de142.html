ou have legal rights that may prevent all or part of your money or property from being taken.  That part of the 
money or property that may not be taken is called “exempt property.”  A partial list of “exempt property” is shown 
below, along with the law which may make all or part of your money or property exempt.  Not withstanding your

f  the  money  or  property  which  is  being  withheld  from  you  includes  any  “exempt  property”,  you  must  file  within 
14days of receiving this Notice of Levy a written claim of exemption with the Clerk of the Court, describing what 
money or property you think is “exempt property” and the reason that it is exempt. 

ou must act quickly to protect your rights. Remember, you only have 14days after receiving this Notice of Levy to 
file your claim of exemption with the Clerk of Court. Your failure to file a claim of exemption with 14days is a waiver 
of your right to file.
