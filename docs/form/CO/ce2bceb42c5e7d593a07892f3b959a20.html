The District Court erred in finding that Goldilocks’s conduct was 

outrageous. Her actions fall below the standard of outrageous conduct,

emotional distress fails.34  

A person is liable when his or her extreme and outrageous 

conduct intentionally or recklessly causes severe emotional distress in 

another. Culpepper v. Pearl St. Bldg., Inc., 877 P.2d 877, 882 (Colo. 1994). 

There are three elements to establish intentional infliction of emotional 

distress: (1) the defendant engaged in extreme and outrageous conduct; 

(2), the defendant engaged in the conduct recklessly or with the intent of

To establish the first element, the conduct must be extreme or

her a room for the night. Id. These actions do not go beyond all possible 

bounds of decency. Although her actions were an unfortunate and 

awkward mistake, they certainly are not atrocious or intolerable.37
