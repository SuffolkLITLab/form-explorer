INSTRUCTIONS TO REGISTER OUT OF STATE GUARDIANSHIP OR 
CONSERVATORSHIP ORDERS IN COLORADO  

These standard instructions are for informational purposes only and do not constitute legal advice about 
your case.  If you choose to represent yourself, you are bound by the same rules and procedures as you 
would be if you were an attorney. 

ENERAL INFORMATION  

  These instructions and forms can be used if you are an out-of-state guardian or conservator, and you want to 
register your out-of-state orders here in Colorado for the purpose of completing specific tasks relating to the 
guardianship and conservatorship in Colorado.  These forms are NOT used for transferring the entire 
guardianship or conservatorship to Colorado.  If you are trying to completely transfer a guardianship or 
conservatorship from out of state to Colorado (because for example, the Ward/Protected Person will be 
moving to Colorado permanently), please refer to the forms and instructions for accepting an adult 
guardianship or conservatorship from the sending state or click here:            
http://www.courts.state.co.us/Forms/Forms_List.cfm?Form_Type_ID=165.  

◆  The registration may be filed by the out-of-state guardian or conservator. 
◆  The  registration  of  an  out-of-state  guardianship  may  be  filed  in  any  appropriate  county  in  Colorado.    The 
registration  of  an  out-of-state  conservatorship  may  be  filed  in  any  Colorado  county  in  which  property 
belonging to the Protected Person is located.  

◆  You  MAY  NOT  register  out-of-state  orders  in  Colorado  if  there  is  a  pending  petition  for  guardianship, 

conservatorship, or other protective orders in Colorado.  

◆  Prior to registering your out-of-state orders in Colorado, you must first give notice to the out-of-state court of 

your intent to register the orders in Colorado.  

◆  Along  with the registration  form, you must attach certified copies of the out-of-state court’s order appointing 
you as guardian or conservator, letters of office or other documents evidencing your authority as guardian or 
conservator, and any bond.   

◆  For additional information, please review §15-14.5-401, §15-14.5-402, and §15-14.5-403, C.R.S.     
◆  If you have a disability and need a reasonable accommodation to access the courts, please contact your local 

ADA Coordinator. Contact information can be obtained from the following website: 
◆  http://www.courts.state.co.us/Administration/HR/ADA/Coordinator_List.cfm
