APPLICATION FOR REGISTERED FOREIGN LANGUAGE 
INTERPRETERS 
 
Instructions 
 

Pursuant to Rule 87 of the Rules of Superintendence for the Courts of Ohio, the 
Language Services Program may maintain and distribute a roster of foreign language 
interpreters for whom the following apply: 

A)  The interpreter is ineligible for certification as a Supreme Court certified foreign 
language interpreter because no oral examination exists or has been developed by the 
National Center for State Courts Language Access Services Section; 

B)  The interpreter demonstrates to the program’s satisfaction proficiency in the 
interpreter’s target language and sufficient preparation to properly interpret case or 
court functions.   

nterpreters in these languages will be registered with the Language Services Program 
and as such, they will be named Registered Foreign Language Interpreter.  To qualify 
for this category and official roster of the Supreme Court of Ohio, the candidate must 
meet the following criteria: 
   

  Be at least eighteen years old 
  Be a citizen, legal resident, or have the legal right to remain and work in the U.S. 
  Have no conviction of any crime involving moral turpitude 
  Attend orientation training before the written exam   
  Pass written examination with a score of 80% or better 
  Attend three courses: Ethics, Modes of Interpretation and Legal Terminology 
  Complete an oral proficiency assessment and score at the top level   
  Sign oath to comply with Code of Ethics for Interpreters and Translators; and 
  Agree to be listed on the official roster. 

o  become  a  registered  foreign  language  interpreter,  please  complete  and  return  this 
application.  The written exam will be administered throughout the year.  Please check 
with the Language Services Program for specific dates.  There is a $125.00 non-refundable 
application fee which includes the written exam, oral assessment testing and the required 
training.

INFORMATION FOR ORAL PROFICIENCY ASSESSMENT: 

Since the certified languages only cover a portion of the total languages used in Ohio 
courts, the Language Services Program under Rule 87 of the Rules of Superintendence 
may maintain and distribute a roster of foreign language interpreters who are ineligible 
for certification because no oral exam is available.  Interpreters in this category are called 
registered Interpreters.  

Registered interpreters are also required to demonstrate proficiency in the interpreter’s 
target and source language as well as sufficient preparation to properly interpret in court. 
An oral proficiency assessment assists the program in making this determination.  

To review a list of languages for oral assessment, please visit the following sites: 

ALTA Language Services  
https://www.altalang.com/language-testing/
