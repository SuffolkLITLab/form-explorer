COURT INTERPRETER CREDENTIALING 
Application for Written Examination 

The Supreme Court of Ohio offers court interpreter credentialing in approximately sixty languages.  To 
see the languages included please visit the links below: 

For certified languages: 
https://www.supremecourt.ohio.gov/courts/services-to-courts/language-services/certification-program/ 

For non-certified or registered languages: 
https://www.altalang.com/language-testing/speakinglisten/ 

As a credentialed court interpreter, you are instrumental in helping the court system carry out its 
mission of access to justice and equal protection.  You will play a key role in helping the courts and legal 
professionals communicate with individuals who are limited English proficient. 

Obtaining your court interpreter credential will show that you are a competent professional and have 
proven language ability to communicate in two languages clearly and accurately.  

As the need for credentialed interpreters continues to grow, you have the opportunity to be part of a 
select group of specially trained professionals who have merged their language skills and their ideals of 
public service in the rewarding career of court interpretation.  To receive credentialing a candidate must 
satisfy each of the following requirements: 

 Be at least eighteen years old

 Be a citizen, legal resident, or have the legal right to remain and work in the United States

 Have no conviction of any crime involving a felony

 Attend orientation training before the written exam

 Pass written examination with a score of 80% or better

 Attend post-written examination training course before the oral exam

 Pass oral examination and/or oral assessment depending on your language and test available

 Sign oath to comply with Code of Ethics for Interpreters and Translators

 Agree to be listed on the roster to work directly with the courts

 Join a professional association if you are a registered interpreter

The LANGAUGE SERVICES SECTION is now accepting applications for the written exam. 
Complete the application and submit it.  Once it is received, you will be scheduled for a mandatory 
Orientation Training.  For more information call 614.387.9404 or visit:  
https://www.supremecourt.ohio.gov/courts/services-to-courts/language-services/certification-program/
