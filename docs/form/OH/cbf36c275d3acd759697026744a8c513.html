Pursuant to Gov. Bar R. X, Sec. 5 (C) and CCLE Reg. 401.2, an attorney or judge may receive CLE credit 
for teaching at an ABA accredited Law School as follows:  
   
 

1.  A  semester  hour  is  defined  as  the  number  of  academic  credit  hours  received  by  a  student  for 

successfully completing a specific higher education course.  

2.  The  Commission  may  allow  three  credit  hours  for  each  semester  hour  of  a  course  taught  by  an 
adjunct or part-time professor at a law school accredited by the American Bar Association the first 
time  the  course  is  taught  by  that  professor  and  one-half  credit  hour  for  each  semester  hour  the 
course is subsequently taught by that professor.  

3.  The  Commission  may  allow  one-half  credit  hour  for  each  semester  hour  taught  by  a  full-time 

professor at a law school accredited by the American Bar Association.  

4.  Prorated credit may be granted for quarter or trimester hours.  

5.  The  Attorney  or  Judge  shall  submit  an  application  for  credit  in  a  manner  authorized  by  the   

Commission within thirty days after the last day of the course.  

6.   An Attorney or Judge shall not receive CLE Credit for hours taught at any other accredited higher 

education institution.
