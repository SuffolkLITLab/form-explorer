INSTRUCTIONS 

Funds withdrawn from an attorney decedent’s Interest on Lawyer’s Trust Account (IOLTA) must be deposited 
into an IOLTA held at an eligible financial institution, registered with the Ohio Access to Justice Foundation 
(Foundation), and held in the name of Applicant or Applicant’s law practice. 

IOLTA funds remain the property of the attorney decedent’s clients . Upon being deposited into an IOLTA in 
the control of Applicant, they must be properly identified and distributed. Under Board Adv. Op. 2008-3, if 
Applicant  is  unable  to  identify  or  locate  the  client  owner  of  the  funds,  the  funds  should  be  reported  and 
remitted to the Ohio Department of Commerce, Division of Unclaimed Funds using the nature of funds code 
TR88 and the appropriate forms: OUF-1A and OUF-2.  

When opening a new IOLTA, the tax ID number to be associated with the account is: 31-1126612. IOLTAs 
must be opened at an eligible financial institution. A complete list of eligible financial institutions can be found 
at: https://www.ohiojusticefoundation.org/lawyers/iolta-iota-financial-institutions/. 

R.C. 4705.09 requires all IOLTAs to be “in the name of the attorney, firm, or association that established and
is maintaining it and shall be identified as an IOLTA or an interest on lawyer’s trust account. The name of
the account may contain additional identifying features to distinguish it from other trust accounts established
and maintained by the attorney, firm, or association.”

An IOLTA established to manage an attorney decedent’s client funds should be identified as an IOLTA and 
include the additional identifying words: “Custodian for attorney decedent’s name.” (e.g. Applicant IOLTA: 
Custodian for the Estate of Jones Law Firm).   

For additional information on IOLTA/IOTA or Attorney Unclaimed Funds, please contact the Foundation at 
ioltaquestions@ohiojusticefoundation.org or 614-715-8560.
