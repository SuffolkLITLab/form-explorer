2021/2023 BIENNIUM CERTIFICATE OF REGISTRATION INSTRUCTIONS
REGISTRATION DEADLINE IS SEPTEMBER 1, 2021
Failure to register timely will subject you to a $50 late fee or suspension from the practice of law.

Rule VI of the Supreme Court Rules for the Government 
of the Bar requires attorneys admitted to and engaged in 
the practice of law in Ohio to register biennially with the 
Supreme Court. The records of the Office of Attorney 
Services indicate that based on your registration status as of 
the date of this mailing, you are required to register for the 
2021/2023 biennium. 

HOW TO REGISTER  You may register online and pay 
your registration fee by credit card or checking account 
(ACH) or by mail and pay the registration fee by check or 
money order. 

To Register Online (Due by 11:59 p.m. EDT, Sept. 1, 2021)

1. Go to the Attorney Services Portal at sc.ohio.gov/
attorneyportal. 

2. Log in using your attorney registration number and 
password. If you do not have a password, click “I forgot my 
password” to have your password emailed to you. 

3. Click “Register.” You may be asked to verify that your 
contact information is up to date. Review and certify all 
information on file is accurate by clicking “Submit and 
Continue to Registration,” which will take you to the 
IOLTA/IOTA screen.

4. All attorneys, regardless of registration status, must 
complete the IOLTA/IOTA Registration (sections 1 and 2) 
before proceeding to the attorney registration screen.

Registration is not complete until you receive an email 
confirmation regarding your 2021/2023 registration status. 
Step-by-step instructions are available at sc.ohio.gov/
AttySvcs/AttyReg/FAQ_onlinereg.asp. 

Online registration is unavailable to first-time emeritus 
pro bono status registrants, who must submit additional 
documentation. Information regarding emeritus pro 
bono status is available at sc.ohio.gov/AttySvcs/AttyReg/
emeritusProBono. 

Do not wait until the last minute to register. By doing so, you 
assume all risk that the online site may not be available and 
you may not be able to complete your registration by the 
deadline. Inability to use the site shall not be an excuse for 
failure to file your registration timely. 

To Register By Mail (Must be postmarked on or before 
Sept. 1, 2021)

1.   Review the Certificate of Registration, marking a single 
line through incorrect information and printing any 
corrections above incorrect items.

2.   Your signature is required on section 5 of the Certificate 

of Registration.

3.   If you have a registration fee, enclose a check or money 
order payable to “The Supreme Court of Ohio.” We are 
unable to accept payment by cash or credit card for mail-
in registrations.

4.   Make a copy of your Certificate of Registration for your 
records and use the enclosed return envelope to submit 
the completed certificate and applicable payment in the 
enclosed envelope to: The Supreme Court of Ohio, PO 
Box 643800, Cincinnati, OH, 45264-3800. 

IOLTA & IOTA INFORMATION  You are required to 
identify all escrow/trust accounts in which the funds of two 
or more clients or third parties have been deposited by you, 
your law firm, or an ancillary business related to the practice 
of law in which you are a principal and to identify whether 
you are a title agent. For each IOLTA/IOTA account, list 
the account number, the financial institution, and the city 
in which the financial institution is located, and indicate 
whether the account is established by you or your firm and 
whether it is an IOLTA or IOTA. If you do not maintain 
an IOLTA or IOTA account, then check the appropriate 
exemption. For questions concerning IOLTA/IOTA, call the 
Ohio Access to Justice Foundation at 614.715.8560.

ATTORNEY REGISTRATION CARDS & 
CONFIRMATION OF REGISTRATION  Approximately 
two weeks after receiving your registration for active status, 
the Office of Attorney Services will mail your registration 
card. Register early to receive your registration card or 
written notification of registration before the new biennium 
begins. If you register for inactive, emeritus pro bono, 
corporate counsel, or military spouse attorney status, you will 
receive written confirmation of your registration. 

CIVIL JUSTICE FUND  Attorneys who register for active, 
corporate, or military attorney spouse status may opt in 
to contribute the $50 voluntary fee to fund civil legal aid 
services for low-income or disadvantaged populations in 
Ohio.

PUBLIC RECORDS  The information you provide 
electronically or on the Certificate of Registration will be 
maintained by the Office of Attorney Services and, except to 
the extent provided in Gov.Bar R. VI(15), is a public record. 
The following are not deemed to be public record: email 
address, service email address, and residence address, unless 
you do not provide a valid office address.
