FORM UJS-126  
REV. 12/2010 

VICTIM’S GUIDE FOR ENFORCING A RESTITUTION ORDER 

eneral Information  

hen a judgment entered in a criminal case orders a defendant to make restitution to a victim of crime, 

payments made by the defendant are collected by the Clerk of Court and forwarded to the victim. 

f the defendant fails to satisfy the restitution as ordered by the court, SDCL 23A-28-1 allows a victim 
to enforce the restitution order in the same manner as a judgment in a civil action. 

he victim may also seek a separate small claims or civil court action against the defendant depending 
on the amount of restitution owed.  If the defendant is a juvenile, the victim may be able to proceed 
against  the  juvenile’s  parents  subject  to  the  limitations  in  SDCL  25-5-15.    Civil  and  Small  Claims 
actions are completely separate actions and do not affect the criminal case in any way. 

he purpose of this brochure is to explain the court process for enforcing a restitution order in the same 
manner as a judgment in a civil action.  This information is not to be confused with legal advice.  If 
you  feel  you  may  need  assistance  with  this  court  process,  you  should  consider  consulting  with  an 
attorney. 

rocedure 

  The  victim  needs  to  file  an  Application  to  Enforce  Restitution  Order  form  (UJS-126)  with  the 
Clerk of Court in the county where the case was prosecuted.  The Clerk of Court will provide the 
form.  No filing fee is required. 

  When filing the Application to Enforce Restitution Order form, the victim must provide the clerk 
of court with a copy of the Order Approving Plan of Restitution, Judgment of Conviction, or Order 
Suspending Imposition of Sentence that spells out the restitution plan as ordered by the court. 

  Once the Application to Enforce Restitution Order is filed, a lien is placed against the defendant’s 
property and a RST docket card showing the amount of restitution the defendant owes the victim is 
created.  The RST docket  card must reflect  the  restitution  order imposed by the court.  Only the 
sentencing court can modify the terms of the restitution order.   

•  The victim can have transcripts of the RST docket card filed in other counties and can request an 
execution be issued in the county where the case is filed or in any county where a transcript of the 
RST docket  card is  filed.  An execution  is  a court order authorizing the  sheriff to  seize property 
belonging  to  the  defendant  and  sell  it  to  satisfy  the  judgment.  There  is  no  guarantee  that  the 
sheriff will be able to locate and sell sufficient property to satisfy the RST docket. 

  Enforced Restitution Orders do not generally expire or get discharged by bankruptcy court.   

  When the defendant makes a payment on the restitution, the clerk will receipt it and will record the 
partial satisfaction on the RST docket card and will disburse the restitution to the victim.  When the 
defendant pays the restitution in full, the clerk will receipt it and will satisfy the RST docket card 
and will disburse the restitution to the victim.    

uestions 

f you have questions about enforcing a restitution order in a case where you are a victim of crime, you 
may contact Tara Hicks at (605) 773-4871 or tara.hicks@ujs.state.sd.us.
