Complete this form in black or blue ink only! 

This is a 5-page form; however, you will need four pages.  You will use either the Affidavit of Mailing 
OR the Admission of Service.  Complete “the Caption.” NOTE:  The caption is the top portion of each 
form.  You will need to know the name of your county, judicial circuit (ask your Clerk of Court if you 
don’t know), name of Plaintiff, name of Defendant and case filing number (ask your Clerk of Court if you 
don’t know).  The caption is the same on every form related to this file. 

Answer or complete the paragraphs within the Notice to Relocate Minor Child(ren): 

•  Paragraphs 1 – 6 are self-explanatory.   

•  Paragraph No. 7:  List the street address, city and state of where you plan to move to.  If you are 

not comfortable with including your street address, you are not required to do so, but you do have to 
include the city and state.  Calculate how many miles away the minor child(ren) would live from the 
other parent, if you were allowed to move. You could use google maps to calculate the distance. 

  
•  Paragraph 8:  Fill in the date that you expect to move.  If you are unable to provide a minimum of 45 
days notice to the other parent, please explain why.  The law does provide that less than 45 days 
notice may be acceptable if the 45 days was “unreasonable under the specific facts giving rise to 
the relocation.”  

•  Paragraph 9:  Explain your reasons for pursuing the proposed relocation with the minor child(ren). 

•  Paragraph 10:  Explain why you feel this move is in the best interests of the minor child(ren). 

•  Paragraph 11:  Presume that the Court allowed you to move with the minor child(ren), what would 

visitation with the other parent look like?  Take into consideration things like:  

Is it possible to continue the current schedule? 

1.  Travel expenses – who will pay what?   
2. 
3.  Do you need a set schedule for FaceTime, phone calls, Skype?  
4.  Will the child(ren) have extra days off school that the other parent can have for visits? 
5.  How do you split holidays? 
6.  How do you divide summer vacations? 
7.  How much advance notice would be needed for travel arrangements? 
8.  Share sports calendars in advance so both parents can attend if possible. 

he more issues you address now, the less you will have to disagree over in the future. The South 

Dakota Parenting Guidelines provides the minimum guidelines if you need some assistance in 
completing this section. 

Do not fill out the verification section until you are in the presence of a notary public or Clerk of 
Court. Make sure to bring photo identification to show the notary public or Clerk of Court. A 
notary public can usually be found at the bank and sometimes at the courthouse.

Once the form is complete, make at least two photocopies of the signed Notice to Relocate Minor 
Child(ren); one for you and one to be served on the other parent. The original copy must be filed with 
the Clerk of Courts. 

ow, you need to get a copy of the Notice to Relocate Minor Child(ren) to the other 

parent and be able to prove to the Court that you sent it to him/her.  This can be 
accomplished a couple different ways: 

f you choose to mail a copy of the Notice to Relocate Minor Child(ren) to the other parent, it must be by 
certified mail, return receipt requested.  You must also complete the Affidavit of Mailing form.  The 
original copy must be filed with the Clerk of Courts with a copy for you and the other parent.  Keep the 
return receipt (aka “green card”) or the return receipt electronic signature (PDF) from the United States 
Postal Service and file with the Clerk of Courts. 

ffidavit of Mailing: 

dmission of Service: 

nstead of mailing this Notice to Relocate Minor Child(ren), you can request that the other parent sign 
an Admission of Service.  This does not indicate that the other parent agrees to the relocation.  It simply 
means that the other parent received the Notice on a specific date.  However, if the other parent 
refuses to sign the Admission of Service, you will need to mail the Notice to him/her. If signed, the 
original Admission of Service must be filed with the Clerk of Courts but keep a copy for yourself and 
give a copy to the other parent.   

ou do not need to do both the Affidavit of Service and Admission of Service. 

ourt-Approved Course Requirement: 

elocation that requires a notice will necessarily impact child custody. Therefore, within sixty days of 

the date of service of the Notice, you must complete a Court-approved parenting course per SDCL 
25-4A-32. This requirement is met if you and the other party have completed a Court-approved course 
within the last five years. The Judge can also waive the requirement, but only for good cause shown. 
You must file an Affidavit on Court-Approved Parenting Course (UJS-364) with the Clerk of Court to 
either provide proof of meeting the requirement or to seek waiver of the requirement. If you seek a 
waiver, file the Affidavit well in advance of the deadline. 

hat’s Next? 

er SDCL 25-4A-19, if the other parent does not agree with your proposed move, he/she may file a 

letter objecting to the move with the Clerk of Courts and may request a hearing.  The objection must be 
made within 30 days of this Notice.  If the other parent fails to request a hearing within 30 days of this 
Notice, the proposed move is presumed to be consented to by them.  Nothing further would be 
required.
