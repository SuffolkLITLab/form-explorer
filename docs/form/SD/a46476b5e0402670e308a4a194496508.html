Initial Referral to Attorney Volunteer 

If your case is accepted for participation 
through Access to Justice, Inc., and you 
are referred to an attorney volunteer, 
the attorney will interview you at your 
initial client meeting.  Thereafter, the 
attorney will make a formal decision as 
to whether or not they can provide you 
with legal assistance/accept your case.  
However, if a volunteer attorney decides 
that your case lacks merit, no further 
referrals will be made.

ABOUT  ACCESS  TO  JUSTICE, 
INC. 

The Access to Justice, Inc., program was set 
up  to  help  South  Dakotaâ€™s two other  legal 
services  programs  provide  legal assistance 
to  eligible  clients,  when  appropriate,  in 
certain  types  of  cases.    Cases  that  are 
accepted  for  participation  through  Access 
to Justice are referred to private attorneys 
who  volunteer  their  time  to  help  clients 
who can not afford to pay an attorney. 

If your case is accepted for participation in 
the Access to Justice pro bono program, an 
attorney volunteer will be asked to consider 
accepting your case.  The process can take 
some  time,  as  Access  to  Justice  staff  may 
need  to  contact  several  attorneys  before 
locating an attorney that has the availability 
to volunteer for your particular case.   

Although Access to Justice staff will try their 
very best to match each accepted case with 
a volunteer attorney; the Access to Justice 
program cannot guarantee that an attorney 
volunteer  will  be  located  for  every  case 
accepted through the program.
