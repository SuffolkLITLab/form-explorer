The  undersigned  is  satisfied  that  the  affidavit  and  any  recorded  testimony  establish  probable  cause  to  believe  that  the  property 
so  described is subject to seizure and/or forfeiture to the State of Alabama, and that grounds exist for the issuance of this Seizure 
Order. 

The  seizing  agency  is  hereby  directed  to  file  this  order  (including  any  and  all  supporting  documentation),  in  accordance  with 
Section  20-2-93(e)(2)a.,  Ala.  Code  1975.  The  Circuit  clerk's  office  shall  create  a  Circuit  Civil  case  number  which  shall  be 
the  case number for any future filings and/or orders related to this matter. 

The  applicant is  hereby  directed,  in  accordance  with  Section  20-2-93(e)(1)b., Ala.  Code 1975,  to 
for forfeiture  and  this  Order (along  with  all  supporting  documents  including  an  inventory)  to the District Attorney’s Office of  this 
Judicial Circuit for review and consideration within 14 days after obtaining this Order.   

submit an application 

Upon a finding of probable cause by the District Attorney that this seizure is valid, the District Attorney may file a forfeiture action 
within 42  days  from  the  date  of  the seizure  of  the  property  (or  longer time upon motion and order of this court for good cause 
shown) pursuant to Sections 20-2-93(f) and (g), Ala. Code 1975. 

Should the District Attorney not file a forfeiture action pursuant to Sections 20-2-93(e)(3) and (f), Ala. Code 1975, after 90 days 
from the date of this  Order, if the  undersigned  judge  dismisses  the  case  due  to  inaction,  the  property  shall be made available by 
the seizing agency for  return  to  the  owner,  upon  documented proof of ownership,  within  14  days  after  the  dismissal  EXCEPT 
(1) upon motion by the District Attorney pursuant to Section 20-2-93(e)(4), Ala. Code 1975, to retain the items as evidence in the
underlying  investigation  or  case,  OR  (2)  if  the  property  is  contraband  in  which  case  it  shall  be  destroyed at  the  conclusion  of
the criminal case, should it not be needed or used as evidence pursuant to Sections 20-2-93(e)(3) and/or (f), Ala. Code 1975.

thereof, shall be sealed by the Clerk of Court, to protect the confidentiality of any ongoing investigation(s) or witness(es).
