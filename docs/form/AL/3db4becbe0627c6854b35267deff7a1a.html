There is no fee to register with AOC’s Foreign Language Interpreter Program. 

o  Upon receipt of the application registration form the interpreter’s name and contact information are added to the 

in-house list that is maintained by the FLIP coordinator  

o  Based on the state comptroller’s pay scale for foreign language interpreters a “registered only interpreter” is paid 

$25 per hour 

•  Upon the FLIP coordinator receiving the complete application registration packet, the authorization for a criminal background 

check and e-verify forms are submitted to the HR division to be processed. 

o  HR notifies the FLIP coordinator of the results of the background and e-verify checks. 
o  The FLIP coordinator then notifies the interpreter of the results.  If both are approved then the interpreter is eligible 

to begin the process to become a certified foreign language interpreter.   

o  The interpreter’s email address is added to the distribution list for interpreters and from that point forward will be 

sent the calendar and registration forms for the orientation seminars, written and oral exams.  

TEP 2: ORIENTATION SEMINAR AND WRITTEN EXAM 

•  Attend one orientation seminar 
• 

Take and pass the written exam with a score of 80% or higher 

o  Upon compliance with the steps one and two of the certification program the interpreter is now “Conditionally 

Approved”. 

o  Although an interpreter must pass the written exam and attend an orientation seminar to be “conditionally 

approved” and to move to the next level of the state pay scale, the interpreter’s name and contact information are 
moved from the in-house list to the list of interpreters on the AOC website once the interpreter has passed the 
written exam.  This list is available to all judges, clerks, attorneys and others in need of a foreign language 
interpreter.  

o  Based on the state comptroller’s pay scale for foreign language interpreters a “conditionally approved interpreter” is 
paid $30 per hour.  Remember you must pass the written exam at 80% or higher and attend an orientation seminar 
before you can move to the next pay level. 

•  NOTE: You must pass the written exam and attend an orientation seminar before you are eligible to take the oral exam.
