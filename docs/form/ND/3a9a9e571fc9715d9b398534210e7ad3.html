INSTRUCTIONS FOR VERIFICATION (PETITION) 
 
ND Legal Self Help Center Staff and Court employees cannot help you fill out forms.  If you are 
unsure how to proceed, consult a lawyer. 

There is no guarantee that all judges and courts will accept forms available through the ND 
Legal Self Help Center.  Use at your own risk. 

Do not include these instruction sheets when you serve or file the completed form. 
 
THE “DISTRICT COURT CIVIL” SECTION of the ND Legal Self Help Center website has additional 
information and resources. 

Verification of a document is a statement of the correctness, truth or authenticity of the 
document by the party submitting the document.   For example, a verified petition has a 
statement of the petitioner that the petition is true. 

In general, a pleading does not need to be verified.  A petition is a pleading.  See Rule 7 of the 
North Dakota Rules of Civil Procedure for a list of documents that are pleadings. 

If a rule or law specifically says a pleading or other document must be verified, verification is 
required. 

Pleadings that must be verified include, but are not limited to, the following: 

• 

• 

• 

Petition for Adoption (See North Dakota Century Code Chapter 14-15) 

Petition for Foreign Adoption (See North Dakota Century Code Chapter 14-15) 

Petition for a Deposition Before the Civil Action is Filed (See Rule 27 of the North 
Dakota Rules of Civil Procedure) 

The signed verification of the document comes directly after the signature on the document 
being verified.  This can be a separate form, as above, or the verification statement and 
signature lines can be copied and pasted after the signature on the document being verified. 

Caption (Top of Verification Form): 

  Complete the caption (top of the Verification form) exactly as it appears in the 

Petitioner’s petition. 

  Fill in the name of the North Dakota county and the name of the judicial district where 
action is or will be filed.  County and judicial district information and maps are available 
at www.ndcourts.gov.

  Fill in the case number, if known.  If the action has not been filed in a North Dakota 

district court, the case number will be assigned by the clerk of court when the action is 
filed. 

Verification Statement: 

  Full, legal name of the party verifying the contents of the document. 

  Fill in the party’s designation.  (Petitioner, Respondent, etc.) 

  Fill in the title of the document the party is verifying.  (Petition, etc.) 

Sign and Date the Verification: 

  The party verifying the contents of the document must sign and date the Verification. 

  The signed Verification of the document comes directly after the signature on the 

document being verified. 

  If a court rule requires the Verification to be notarized, the individual giving their written 
statement of fact must sign and date the verification in the presence of a notary public 
or clerk of court.  The notary public or clerk of court will witness the signature and 
indicate this on the document. 

Do not include these instruction sheets when you serve or file the completed form.
