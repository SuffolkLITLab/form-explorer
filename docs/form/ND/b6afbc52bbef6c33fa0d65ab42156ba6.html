Preparing for Your Remote Hearing 

Court Decorum 
Remember that, even though you are not physically in a courtroom, you are asked to present yourself as though you 
are. Part of court decorum includes being prepared, limiting distractions, speaking respectfully, and wearing what you 
would normally wear to court. 

If you have any special needs, contact the court prior to the hearing date. 

 Be Prepared  
• 
•  Get to know your case. 
•  During your hearing, have copies nearby of anything you or anyone else filed in your case. 
•  Test the device you will use for your remote hearing. You will need to know:  

o  Where to join so you’ll have a good internet connection. 
o  How to turn off and on your video. 
o  How to mute and unmute your audio. 
o  How to rename yourself in Zoom to show your name. 
o  How to apply a virtual background (if your background may be distracting). 

imiting Distractions 
•  Turn off anything creating background noise, such as TVs, music, and phone notifications. 
•  Stay safe - do NOT drive during the hearing. 
•  Make arrangements to limit interruptions by children or pets. 
•  Stay in one place when your camera is on so everyone can see you. 
•  Connect from a private room where you can shut the door. 
• 

If you do not have a safe or distraction-free area to use for your hearing, check with friends, family for quiet spaces.
