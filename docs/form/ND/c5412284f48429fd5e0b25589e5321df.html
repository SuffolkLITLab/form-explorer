Instructions to Request a Contempt Citation for Child Support Arrears 

IMPORTANT!  READ BEFORE USING THESE FORMS AND INSTRUCTIONS 

ND Legal Self Help Center staff and court employees can’t help you fill out any forms. 

If you’re unsure how to proceed, or need legal advice or legal representation, consult a lawyer 
licensed to practice in North Dakota. 

To protect your rights, carefully read this information and any instructions to which you’re 
referred. 

When you represent yourself, you’re expected to know and follow the law, including: 

State or federal laws that apply to your case;

•
• Case law, also called court opinions, that applies to your case; and
• Court rules that apply to your case, which may include:
o North Dakota Rules of Civil Procedure;
o North Dakota Rules of Court;
o North Dakota Rules of Evidence;
o North Dakota Administrative Rules and Orders; and
o Any local court rules.

Links to the state laws, case law, and court rules can be found at www.ndcourts.gov. 

When you represent yourself, you’re held to the same requirements and 
responsibilities as a lawyer, even if you don’t understand the rules or procedures.  If 
you’re unsure if these forms and instructions suit your circumstances, consult a lawyer. 

•

If you would like to learn more about finding a lawyer to represent you, go to
www.ndcourts.gov/legal-self-help/finding-a-lawyer.

A glossary with definitions of legal terms is available at www.ndcourts.gov/legal-self-help. 

These instructions and forms aren’t a complete statement of the law.  They cover the basic 
process for asking the North Dakota state district court to issue a contempt of court citation when 
an individual required to pay child support is past due.  There’s no guarantee that all judges and 
courts will accept forms available through the ND Legal Self Help Center.  The Center isn’t 
responsible for any consequences that may result from the forms or information provided. 

Use at your own risk. 

Don’t include these instructions when you serve or file the completed form(s).

Purpose 

If a North Dakota state district court issued a child support order and the individual required to 
pay is past due with payments (in arrears), you may ask the district court to find them in 
contempt of court. 

If you’re owed past due child support, you complete the forms.  The forms are filed in your 
existing North Dakota State District Court child support case. 

If you’re unsure how to proceed or unsure if these forms are suitable for your situation, consult 
an attorney. 

Where to File 

The forms are filed in the district court of the North Dakota county where your existing child 
support case is filed. 

•

•

If child support was ordered as part of a divorce, legal separation, paternity, custody, or
guardianship case, file your forms in that existing case number.

If child support was ordered as its own case, file your forms in that existing case
number.

You MUST file in the district court of the North Dakota county where your child support case is 
filed, even if you no longer live in that county. 

However, if the North Dakota State District Court notified you that your child support case was 
moved to a different North Dakota county, file in the district court of the new county. 

Contact information for Clerks of District Court by North Dakota county is available at 
ndcourts.gov/court-locations. 

Definitions 

Arrears – An overdue payment, or payments.  If the Obligor is ordered by the court to pay child 
support and hasn’t paid as ordered, the Obligor has child support arrears. 

Contempt of Court – In general, contempt of court is disobedience of a court order. 

IV-D Child Support Case – “IV-D case” is a reference to Title IV-D of the Social Security Act which
created the federal Child Support program.  An IV-D case is when the North Dakota Child
Support program provides full services after receiving 1) an application for services from one of
the parents; 2) a referral to service the case from a public assistance program (TANF, foster
care, and Medicaid); or 3) a request for assistance from another state’s Child Support program.

Notice of Arrears – A written notice from a Clerk of District Court to an Obligor that they are 
past due on their court-ordered child support and includes the amount of the arrears. 

Obligee – The person receiving child support ordered by the court.  If you’re filling out these 
forms because you’re owed child support payments ordered by a North Dakota state district 
court, you’re the Obligee. 

Obligor – The person ordered by the court to pay child support. 

Order to Show Cause – A written demand of a judge or judicial referee for an Obligor to appear 
at a hearing to justify, explain or prove why they shouldn’t be held in contempt of court for 
disobeying the order to pay child support. 

Two-Part Process to Request a Contempt Citation 

Part One requires you to complete and file the Request for Notice of Arrears form with the 

Clerk of District Court.  The Clerk of District Court generates a written Notice of Arrears and 
sends it to the Obligor. 

Part Two is only necessary when the Obligor doesn’t pay the arrears in full after receiving the 
Notice of Arrears from Part One.  If the Obligor doesn’t pay, Part Two requires you to complete 
and file the Request for Contempt Citation form with the Clerk of District Court.  The Clerk of 
District Court serves an Order to Show Cause with a hearing date and time on the Obligor. 

Part One: Request for Notice of Arrears 

When an Obligor is overdue paying court-ordered child support payments, Section 14-09-
08.1(3) of the North Dakota Century Code allows the Obligee to ask the Clerk of District Court 
to send notice of the arrears to the Obligor.

Step 2: File the Original, Completed Form with the Clerk of Court  

File the original, completed Request for Notice of Arrears form with the Clerk of District Court

•

•

If child support was ordered as part of a divorce, legal separation, paternity, custody, or

If child support was ordered as its own case, file your forms in that case number.

You MUST file in the district court of the North Dakota county where your child support case is 
filed, even if you no longer live in that county. 

Contact information for Clerks of District Court by North Dakota county is available at 
ndcourts.gov/court-locations. 

If the Clerk of District Court accepts your form for filing, the Clerk of Court will generate a 
Notice of Arrears and send it to the Obligor.  You’ll receive a copy, as well. 

Part Two: Request for Contempt Citation 

If the Obligor doesn’t pay the full arrears after receiving the Notice of Arrears from the Clerk of 
District Court, Section 14-09-08.1(3) of the North Dakota Century Code allows the Obligee to 
ask for a contempt citation.

Step 1: You, the Obligee, Complete the Request for Contempt Citation  

Request for Contempt Citation form: 

•

Top of Form (Caption)

o Complete the caption exactly as you completed the caption of the Request for

Notice of Arrears form in Part One.

• Carefully Read and Complete Paragraphs 1 through 5 of the Form

• Date and Signature

o Date and sign the form.
o Complete the lines following the signature line.

• Make 1 Copy of the Completed Form

o Keep the copy for your records.

Step 2: File the Original, Completed Form with the Clerk of Court  

File the original, completed Request for Contempt Citation form with the Clerk of District Court 
of the North Dakota State District Court where you filed your Request for Notice of Arrears 
form in Part One. 

If the Clerk of District Court accepts your form for filing, the Clerk of District Court will serve an 
Order to Show Cause, with the date, time and location of the hearing, on the Obligor. 

•

•

You, the Obligee, will be notified of the date, time and location of the hearing by the
Clerk of District Court.

If your case is an IV-D Child Support case, the Clerk of Court will provide a copy to the
North Dakota Child Support program.

Step 3: You, the Obligee, Appear at the Hearing  

At the hearing, the judge or judicial referee will require the Obligor to justify, explain or prove 
why they shouldn’t be held in contempt of court for disobeying the court order to pay child 
support.

Arrive Early to the Courthouse on the Date of the Hearing: 

If your hearing is in-person at the courthouse, arrive early so you don’t miss your hearing date 
and time. If you have a serious, unavoidable reason you can’t get to court on the date and time 
scheduled for trial, call the District Court as soon as you can. 

If the Hearing will by Held by Zoom, Make Sure You Can Connect to the Zoom Hearing: 

If your hearing is held electronically by Zoom, well before the hearing begins, make sure you 
can connect and understand how to function in Zoom. 

Don’t be late to the Zoom hearing. If you have a serious, unavoidable reason you can’t attend 
the hearing on the date and time scheduled for trial, call the District Court as soon as you can. 

At the end of hearing, the judge or judicial referee will decide whether to find the Obligor in 
contempt of court.  If the judge or judicial referee finds the Obligor in contempt of court, the 
judge or judicial referee will then decide how the Obligor will fix the contempt. 

You’ll receive a copy of the signed and dated decision by mail or email. 

Don’t include these instructions when you serve or file the completed forms.
