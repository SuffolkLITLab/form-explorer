Can a Guardian get paid for providing their guardianship services? 

Maybe, if your request is reasonable under the circumstances.  Guardians appointed by a North 
Dakota state district court may receive payment for their guardianship services to the Ward.  The 
Guardian must get approval from the judge or judicial referee assigned to the guardianship case. 

The judge or judicial referee assigned to the guardianship case decides what a guardianship service 
is, and whether the amount you request for payment is reasonable under the circumstances. 

Fee approval forms are available on the ND Legal Self Help Center webpage to make the request.

What may the court consider when deciding whether to approve payment to a 
Guardian for their guardianship services? 

The judge or judicial referee assigned to the guardianship case must decide if your request is 
reasonable under the circumstances, and may consider the following: 

•  Whether your fees for providing direct guardianship services are reasonable, well documented, 

and related to your guardianship duties. 
•  The size and nature of the Ward’s estate. 
•  The Ward’s anticipated future needs. 
•  The benefit to the Ward, or the Ward’s estate, of your guardianship services. 
•  The necessity of the guardianship services you performed. 
•  The time you spent performing the guardianship services. 
•  Whether the guardianship service you performed was routine or required more than ordinary 

•  Whether you brought any unusual skill, expertise, or experience to the performance of your 

•  Your experience, reputation, diligence and ability related to the guardianship service you 

•  Your estimate of the value of the guardianship services you performed. 
•  The fee customarily charged in the community for similar services. 
•  The nature and length of your relationship with the Ward. 
•  Any conflict of interest you may have related to the guardianship service you performed. 
•  Whether your appointment as Guardian prevents you from other employment. 

NOTE:  The judge or judicial referee assigned to the guardianship case isn’t limited to the list above.  

They may consider other things that aren’t included on this list when deciding if your request for 
payment is reasonable under the circumstances.
