Frequently Asked Questions About 
Conservatorship Review Hearings 

What is a Conservatorship Review Hearing? 

When a North Dakota state district court judge or judicial referee appoints a 
conservator for an adult or a minor child, the conservatorship must be periodically 
reviewed by the judge or judicial referee to decide if the conservatorship should: 

1.  Continue with the current conservator(s); 
2.  Continue with a different conservator or conservators; or 
3.  End (terminate).  

How Often is a Conservatorship Review Hearing Held? 

Conservatorship orders can last no more than 5 years without a review hearing. Review 
hearings must be held no later than 5 years from the order appointing a conservator. 

How Will I Know When the Conservatorship Review Hearing Will be Held? 

At least 90 days before the order appointing a conservator expires, the District Court 
mails the notice of the review hearing with the date, time and location. 

If you’re the conservator, you’ll likely receive an “Information Related to Continuation of 
Conservatorship” form to fill out and return to the court as soon as possible. The notice 
of the review hearing should have the deadline to return the form. 

Contact the Clerk of Court if you don’t know the deadline to return the form. 

What if it’s Difficult to Attend the Hearing In-Person at the Courthouse? 

You can make a written request to ask for any of the following: 

•  To hold the hearing remotely, instead of in-person at the courthouse. 
•  To allow you, the adult subject to conservatorship, or others to attend remotely. 
•  To hold the in-person hearing at a different location, instead of the courthouse. 
•  To allow the adult subject to conservatorship to not attend the hearing at all. 

Go to Requests Related to Attending Conservatorship Hearings for request forms.

What Happens at the Conservatorship Review Hearing? 

The conservator(s), the person subject to the conservatorship, and other interested 
people tell the District Court judge or judicial referee why they believe the 
conservatorship should or should not continue. 

If you have any documents you didn’t write that you want the judge or judicial referee 
to consider, you must have the person who wrote the document at the hearing. 

If you want the judge or judicial referee to hear what someone else has to say about the 
conservatorship, that person must be at the hearing. You can’t tell the judge or the 
judicial referee what that person said or has to say. 

After the hearing, the judge or judicial referee will decide if the conservatorship should: 

1)  Continue with the current conservator(s); 
2)  Continue with different conservator(s); or 
3)  End (terminate). 

Can I Ask for Modifications to the Conservatorship at the Review Hearing? 

Yes, the review hearing is also your chance to tell the judge or judicial referee about 
changes you believe would benefit the person subject to the conservatorship. 

The judge or judicial referee may order modifications during the review hearing, or may 
require you to ask for modifications by making a formal, written motion. 

If you’re required to make a formal, written motion to ask for modifications, go to 
ndcourts.gov/legal-self-help/conservatorship. There may be a form set available. 

If you don’t find a form set for your request, contact a lawyer for help. 

Is the Conservator Still Required to File and Serve an Annual Report? 

Yes. The annual report requirement is separate from the review hearing requirements.  
Each conservator is required to prepare, serve and file an annual report by the due date 
in the most recent order appointing a conservator. 

If a conservator isn’t up to date on their annual report requirement, they should be 
prepared to explain why at the review hearing.
