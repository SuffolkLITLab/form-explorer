ND LEGAL SELF HELP CENTER COMMONLY REFERENCED NORTH DAKOTA 
STATUTES AND COURT RULES (Civil Actions Only) 

 

Both attorneys and self-represented (pro-se) litigants are required to know and follow all statutes, court rules 
and procedures.  The following statutes and rules are starting points for research.  Other statutes or rules may 
apply.  Attorneys and self-represented litigants should not rely solely on the information provided in this guide. 

Statutes are the laws passed by the North Dakota State Legislature.  North Dakota statutes are found in the 
North Dakota Century Code or N.D.C.C.  (www.legis.nd.gov)  

Court rules are approved and adopted by the North Dakota Supreme Court.  Court rules govern the procedure 
in a court action.  Or, to put it another way, control how an action moves through the court system.  In civil 
actions, the commonly used court rules are: (www.ndcourts.gov/legal-resources/rules)  

North Dakota Supreme Court opinions, also called case law, may affect the interpretation of statutes and rules.

•  North Dakota Rules of Civil Procedure 
•  North Dakota Rules of Court 
•  North Dakota Rules of Evidence
