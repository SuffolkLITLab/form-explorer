INSTRUCTIONS FOR VERIFICATION FORM 

ND Legal Self Help Center Staff and Court employees can’t help you fill out forms.  If you’re 
unsure how to proceed, consult a lawyer. 

There’s no guarantee that all judges and courts will accept forms available through the ND 
Legal Self Help Center.  Use at your own risk. 

Don’t include these instruction sheets when you serve or file the completed form. 

THE “GUARDIANSHIPS AND CONSERVATORSHIPS” SECTION – “Guardianship of Adults” link of 
the ND Legal Self Help Center website has additional information and resources. 

Verification of a document is a statement of the correctness, truth or authenticity of the 
document by the party submitting the document.   For example, a verified petition has a 
statement of the petitioner that the petition is true. 

The general rule is a pleading isn’t required to be verified.  A petition is a pleading.  See Rule 7 
of the North Dakota Rules of Civil Procedure for a list of legal documents that are pleadings. 

However, if a rule or law specifically says a pleading or other document must be verified, 
verification is required.   

Review the rules or laws that apply to your legal document to determine if your document must 
be verified.  Look for words in the rule or law like “verified petition,” or “petition must be 
verified.” 

Pleadings that must be verified include, but aren’t limited to, the following: 

Petition for Adoption (See North Dakota Century Code Chapter 14-15) 

Petition for a Deposition Before the Civil Action is Filed (See Rule 27 of the North 
Dakota Rules of Civil Procedure) 

Petition for Domestic Violence Protection Order (See North Dakota Century Code 
Chapter 14-07.1) 

Petition to Establish Date and Place of Birth (See North Dakota Century Code 
Chapter 32-37) 

Petition for Nonparent Custody or Visitation (See North Dakota Century Code 
Chapter 14-09.4)

If a rule or law says your legal document must be verified, you have three options: 

1.  Use a separate verification form (as above), which you attach to your legal document as 

the last page; 

2.  Copy and paste the verification statement and the date and signature block from the 
form above directly after the date and signature block on the legal document; or 

3.  Copy and paste the verification statement as the last paragraph of your legal document, 
AND use the verification date and signature block as the date and signature block of 
your legal document. 

Caption (Top of Verification Form): 

(ONLY fill in the caption IF you plan to use option 1 above.  If you plan to use option 2 or 3 
above, skip this step.) 

  Complete the caption (top of the Verification form) exactly as it appears in the 

Petitioner’s petition. 

  Fill in the name of the North Dakota county and the name of the judicial district where 
action is or will be filed.  County and judicial district information and maps are available 
at ndcourts.gov.  

  Fill in the case number, if known.  If the action has not been filed in a North Dakota 

district court, the case number will be assigned by the clerk of court when the action is 
filed. 

Verification Statement: 

(If you plan to use option 2 or 3 above, copy and paste the verification statement into your legal 
document and fill in the blanks using the following checkboxes.) 

  Full, legal name of the party verifying the contents of the document. 

  Fill in the party’s designation.  (Petitioner, Respondent, etc.) 

  Fill in the title of the document the party is verifying.  (Petition, etc.) 

Sign and Date the Verification Statement: 

(If you plan to use option 2 or 3 above, copy and paste the date and signature block from the 
Verification form into your legal document and fill in the blanks using the following checkboxes.) 

  The self-represented party must date and sign the verification statement.
