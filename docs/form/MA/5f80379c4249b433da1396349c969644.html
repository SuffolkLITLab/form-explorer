DEFENDANT (S) CRIMINAL CLAIM OF APPEAL (To be read and delivered to defendant and counsel) Having been found guilty, you have the right to appeal to the Appeals Court . of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts within twenty (20) days. (If the sentence is not imposed on the same day as the Verdict or Finding, the defendant shall be . . informed that an appeal shall be claimed within twenty (20) . days after the judgment) . You must file a Notice of Appeal with the Clerk of the Housing Court . . within twenty (20) days after the entry of judgment. The . . Notice of Appeal must specify the name of the defendant and must designate with . . particularity, the judgment or Order of part thereof which is being appealed. . . The Clerk will notify the Commonwealth that a Notice of Appeal has been filed. . You may, upon a showing of excusable neglect, be granted an extension of thirty . . (30) days in which to file a Notice of Appeal. It is your responsibility (if attorney present in action, attorney is obligated to act if so advised by client) to order from the Court, those portions . of the transcript which are designated both by you and the Commonwealth. You may . order at transcript of the entire proceeding. If less than the entire transcript . . is requested you must, within ten (10) days after filing the Notice of Appeal, . file in the Housing Court Department, Division, and serve on . the Commonwealth, a description of the parts of the transcript you have ordered . . or intend to order and a statement of the issues which you intend to present on . . appeal. If no . transcript of the proceedings is available, you may prepare a statement of the evidence and proceedings and serve that on the Commonwealth. . . The Commonwealth has ten (10) days to object to, or . propose amendments to the statement; the statement must then be submitted to the Housing Court Department, . . Division, for approval and inclusion in the Record.
