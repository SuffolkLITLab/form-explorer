INSTRUCTIONS TO THE JUDGMENT CREDITOR (PLAINTIFF):  Supplementary process is a procedure used to bring into 
court a judgment debtor who has refused to voluntarily pay a civil money judgment in order to examine the judgment debtor's 
ability to pay the judgment and to seek a court order to compel payment.  See Mass. Gen. Laws c. 224, §14. 
  
A supplementary process proceeding may be brought in the District Court or Boston Municipal Court division where the 
judgment debtor lives or has a usual place of business or employment.  However, if this proceeding is incidental to a case or 
matter in a Housing Court division then it must be brought in said Housing Court division.  If applicable, the Fair Debt 
Collection Practices Act, 15 U.S.C.A. §§1692 et seq., may contain additional requirements that a judgment creditor must 
comply with before commencing a supplementary process proceeding. 
  
To begin this procedure, this application must be completed and then filed with the clerk's office along with the required entry 
fee.  You  do  not  have  to,  but  you  may  file  the  original  or  a  copy  of  the  order  of  judgment  or  execution  with  this  application. 
However, at the supplementary process hearing, you will be required to present the original execution or other certified proof 
of  the  unpaid  money  judgment.    Upon  the  payment  of  the  required  entry  fee,  the  clerk's  office  will  issue  a  summons  under 
court seal to you to be served on the judgment debtor at least 7 days before the hearing date noted on the summons.
