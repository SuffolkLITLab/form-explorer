______________________________________________________________________________ 

Pursuant to G. L. c. 261, § 27B, the Chief Justice of the Supreme Judicial Court 
prescribes the "affidavit of indigency" form that is used in determinations regarding court costs 
of indigent persons.  As you know, the form follows the language of G. L. c. 261, § 27A, 
providing three different definitions of the word "indigent."  The second definition is "a person 
whose income, after taxes, is one hundred and twenty-five per cent or less of the current poverty 
threshold established annually by the "Community Services Administration . . ."1 

The following table is based on the new Federal Poverty Guidelines, as they appear in the 
Federal Register of January 19, 2023, Volume 88, No. 12, at pages 3424 - 3425.  By multiplying 
each figure in the Federal Register table by 125%, one gets the following guidelines for use 
pursuant to G. L. c. 261, § 27A.
