Rent and Utilities:  

COVID-19: Help for West Virginians 

Mountaineer Rental Assistance Program (MRAP) – www.wvhdf.com or call 211 
If you are eligible, MRAP provides financial help to low-income renters struggling to pay rent 
or utility payments because of the impact of COVID-19. Help can be applied to past due rent 
and/or utilities dating back to 4/1/20 and up to 3 months future rent. Apply online with the 
WV Housing Development Fund or dial 211 for a paper application or if you have questions. 

Temporary Order Stopping Eviction for Non-Payment of Rent:  

CDC Order- https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/covid-eviction-declaration.html  
To stop the spread of COVID-19, this is a federal order that can protect renters who are being 
evicted for not being able to pay rent if they meet certain conditions. If eligible, the order 
stops evictions until June 30, 2021.  You must submit a Declaration Form to your landlord. You 
will still owe unpaid rent. Go to the CDC website for the order and Declaration Form.  

Internet: 

Emergency Broadband Benefit- https://getemergencybroadband.org/ or call 1-833-511-0311 
This program provides a discount of up to $50 a month to help low-income households pay for 
internet service. A one-time discount of up to $100 for a laptop, tablet or computer is available. 
It is a temporary program that will end when the funds are used. More information on eligibility 
and the application can be found at the website or by calling.  

Help if you lost your job or have reduced wages: 
          Unemployment Compensation- https://workforcewv.org/ or 1-800-252-JOBS 

Unemployment provides temporary financial help to individuals who have lost their job through 
no fault of their own. During COVID-19 the program has been expanded to (1) cover people who 
are  self-employed,  (2)  cover  people  for  a  longer  time  and  (3)  provides  a  higher  amount  of 
financial help than normal. These extra benefits end 6/19/21 in WV. Apply online or call. 

Stimulus Check:  

Economic Impact Payments- https://www.irs.gov/coronavirus/economic-impact-payments  
The  IRS  sent  out  payments  in  April/May  2020  ($1,200  for  individual),  Dec.  2020/Jan.  2021 
($600), and March/April 2021 ($1,400) to eligible households, with different amounts/rules for 
children and dependents. If you did not receive payments, you should file taxes this year even 
after the tax deadline. Claim the Recovery Rebate Credit on your tax return. Even if you have 
no  income,  you  are  entitled  to  payment.  Find  free  tax  help:  In  person:  VITA- 
https://irs.treasury.gov/freetaxprep/    Online:  https://www.irs.gov/filing/free-file-do-your-
federal-taxes-for-free   

•  Food Stamps (SNAP)- Apply: https://www.wvpath.org 

Provides  nutrition  benefits  to  low-income  families  so  they  can  purchase  food.  To  help  get 
through COVID-19, SNAP is providing an additional $28 per person each month until 9/30/21.  

•  P-EBT- https://wvde.us/wv-pebt/ (Don’t need to apply) or 304-756-5431 

Provides nutrition assistance on an EBT card that can be used to purchase groceries for families 
with school children who qualify for free/reduced lunch when school is out of session due to 
COVID-19. Many counties in West Virginia automatically qualify for P-EBT for all students. This

Health Insurance:  

• 

Insurance Marketplace- Help with Premiums: https://www.healthcare.gov/ or 1-800-318-2596 
If you do not qualify for Medicaid, you can purchase health insurance through the Marketplace, 
and get tax credits to help lower your premium. To help get through COVID-19, more people 
qualify for tax credits for health insurance purchased through the Marketplace. Some people 
may not have to pay any premiums through 2022. Open enrollment extended through 8/15/21.  

•  COBRA Premium Help (If you lost your job or had work hours cut): 

COBRA allows certain people to extend employer provided health insurance when they lose 
their job/cut work hours. To help get through COVID-19, people may be eligible for help with 
health premiums if they take COBRA continuation coverage. This help is available from 4/1/21 
through 9/30/21. You should get a notice from your employer about your eligibility.  

•  COVID-19  Vaccines  are  Free!  Sign  up:  1-833-734-0965  or  https://dhhr.wv.gov/covid-

19/pages/vaccine.aspx  
The federal government is  providing the COVID-19 vaccine for free, even if you don’t have 
health insurance. You cannot be charged for the vaccine or administration costs.  

Fully Refundable Tax Credits- Money for You and Your Family: 

Expanded Child Tax Credit: https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/advance-child-tax-credit-
payments-in-2021  
For 2021, the Child Tax Credit has been expanded to help parents with children.  If you are 
eligible for the full credit, the IRS will refund you $3,600 for kids 0-5 years old and $3,000 for 
kids 6-17. Reduced payments for higher incomes. For some, partial payments of the credit will 
be made monthly starting in July 2021. The rest will be refunded when you file 2021 taxes, in 
2022. You do not need to have income from work or benefits to get this credit. File taxes to 
get  the  credit.  Find  free  tax  help:  In  person:  VITA-  https://irs.treasury.gov/freetaxprep/ 
Online: https://www.irs.gov/filing/free-file-do-your-federal-taxes-for-free   

Help with Funeral Costs:  

COVID-19 Funeral Assistance: https://www.fema.gov/disasters/coronavirus/economic/funeral-
assistance or 1-844-684-6333 
FEMA is providing financial help for COVID-19 related funeral expenses that were incurred after 
1/20/20.  You will need to provide documentation showing that the death was attributed to 
COVID-19 and proof of expenses. Apply by calling. More information on eligibility is online. 

  
Find Local Help:  
 

Dial 211:  https://www.wv211.org/  
Many local organizations are offering help with food, housing assistance and more. Dial 211 or 
go to their website to find more information about assistance that may be available to help 
you in your community.
