The Connecticut Superior Court issued a decree dated___________________resulting in the federal firearms disability. 
(List the court, the type of matter, the date of the adjudication and the date the order was terminated or expired.) 

Not including the Probate Court or Superior Court order above, list any court orders that affected the petitioner’s right

The petitioner is not otherwise ineligible or legally barred from purchasing, possessing or transporting firearms or 
ammuition under the laws of Connecticut or under the equivalent laws of any other state or under federal law.   
As required by law, the following documents are also attached to this petition:

1. Releases for all of the petitioner’s records related to the petition, including health, mental health, military,
immigration, juvenile court, civil court, criminal court records and any other of the petitioner’s records that may relate to
the petition. These releases authorize the Commissioner of Emergency Services and Public Protection to obtain any of
these records for use at the Probate Court hearing or in any appeal from the Probate Court’s decision. (Attach Release of
Information Re Petition to Restore Right to Purchase, Possess or Transport a Firearm or Ammunition, PC-150.)

2. Certified copies of medical records detailing the petitioner's psychiatric history where applicable, including
records pertaining to the specific adjudication or commitment that is the subject of this application;

3. Certified copies of medical records from all of the petitioner's current treatment providers, if the petitioner is receiving
treatment;

4. A certified copy of all criminal history information maintained on file by the State Police Bureau of Identification and
the Federal Bureau of Investigation pertaining to the petitioner or a copy of the response from said bureaus indicating
that there is no criminal history information on file; and

5. Evidence of the petitioner's reputation, which may include notarized letters of reference from current and past
employers, family members or personal friends, affidavits from the petitioner or other character evidence.

All required information listed above must accompany the petition at the time it is submitted to the court. Information pro- 
vided after receipt of this petition will not be considered unless submitted in response to a specific request from the court. 

Any information specifically requested by the court must be certified copies of original documents and must be received 
by the court no later than 15 days after the date of the request. The court may extend this time period for good cause 
shown. Failure to provide the requested information within such time period may result in a denial of this petition. 

WHEREFORE, the petitioner requests that the court approve this petition to restore the petitioner’s right to purchase, 
possess or transport a firearm or ammunition pursuant to C.G.S. section 45a-100. 

The representations made in this petition are made under penalty of false statement.
