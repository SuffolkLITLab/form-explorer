Q. I need a lawyer. Where can I get one?
A. If  you were arrested and cannot pay an attorney to represent 
you, you may apply for a Public Defender at their office on 
the first floor. If you make too much to get help from a Public 
Defender, you may want to call the Lawyer Referral Service 
of  the New Haven County Bar Association at (203) 562-5750 
or check the yellow pages for an attorney. In addition, to 
find out if  you can get help from Statewide Legal Services 
of  Connecticut, please call 1-800-453-3320 (English and 
Spanish) Monday-Friday, 9 a.m. - 3 p.m.

Q. How do I get back the money I put up as a bond?
A. Money paid to a bondsperson is not refundable. If you 
put up a cash bond, the case must be disposed (closed) or 
the defendant must be in a diversionary program before the 
clerk’s office can return the bond money. The clerk will need 
the original receipt for the money you put up. If your original 
receipt is lost, you must fill out an Affidavit of Lost Bond 
Receipt (available at the Clerk’s Office). You must also show 
a photo ID. The clerk’s office will issue a check (not cash) to 
you for the amount of money you put up as a bond.

If you have been arrested and you do not have an attorney and a 
Public Defender has not been appointed to represent you, please 
talk to the State’s Attorney (prosecutor) in the courtroom.

Q. I want to start a lawsuit. Where do I go? 
A. The Public Information Desk in the lobby on the first floor 
can give you court forms and information about the fees to 
file lawsuits. The staff  cannot give you legal advice. To help 
you with your research, you may want to go to the law library 
located on the 7th floor at 235 Church Street, which has forms, 
books and copies of  court rules. 

Q. How do I open an old traffic ticket?
A. You must pay a $60 fee to the clerk’s office by cash, 
money order, or credit card (checks are not accepted). The clerk’s 
office will give you a court date and instructions on how to get 
your driver’s license back. If your license is suspended you will 
have to pay a fee to the DMV to get your license back. 

Q. How do I pay my fine? 
A. You may pay fines and fees at the clerk’s office on or before 
the due date by cash, credit card, money order or personal 
check payable to Clerk, Superior Court. Personal checks must 
be pre-printed with your current name and address. A valid 
picture ID is required. Checks cannot be more than $800.

Q. How do I bail someone out of  jail?
A. After the court sets the amount of  the bond (bail) you can 
do one of  three things:
	You may put up a cash bond, which means you must pay 
	
	
the exact amount of  the bond in cash to the clerk’s office 
or, if  approved by the Court, you may put up 10 percent 
of  the surety bond set.

	You may hire a bail bondsperson who will charge you  
	
	
a fee based on the total amount of  the bond required.

	You may put up a real estate bond.
	
	

Q. How can I get child support?
A. The State can help you get a child support order or 
enforce an order that you already have. You may contact 
the Department of  Social Services in Hartford at (860) 723-
1002. You also may talk with an attorney or try to get child 
support on your own. The Court Service Center, 235 Church 
Street first floor, has forms and will help you fill them out.

Q. Where do I go for a restraining order to stop  
family violence?
A. The Clerk’s Office at 235 Church Street (2nd floor).

Q. I want to talk to a judge or magistrate about  
my case. Who is available? 
A. The Code of  Judicial Conduct does not allow a judge 
or magistrate to speak to you about any civil or criminal  
case that has started or any lawsuit you want to start. Judges 
and magistrates will listen to your comments only at a 
scheduled hearing.

Q. Where is a public copier?
A. Photocopies can be made at the Public Information  
Desk located in the lobby on the 1st floor.

Q. Is there a Notary Public in the building? 
A. Yes, at the Public Information Desk and there is no fee.

Q. How do I file a will, search land records, get a birth 
certificate or file a lien?
A. Contact the correct Probate Court, Registrar of  Vital 
Statistics, or Town Clerk’s Office.

Q. Where do I find a State Marshal to serve my papers?
A. You may get a list from the Public Information Desk on 
the first floor lobby. There is also a State Marshal on duty  
at 235 Church Street from 12:30-1:00 and 4:30-5:00 daily.
