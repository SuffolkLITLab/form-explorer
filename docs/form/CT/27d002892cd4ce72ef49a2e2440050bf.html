Q:	How	will	I	be	notified	that	I	 
 

am excused?

A:	You	will	be	notified	via	United	States		
  mail that you have been excused for   
one year due to extreme hardship.
 

Q: Is this excuse permanent?

A: No. You may be randomly selected
to serve again during the following
 
court year.  If this occurs, you must
 
re-apply to be excused for extreme  
 
hardship. A court year runs from
 
September 1 through August 31 in
 
any given year.
 

 

Q: Where can I get more information?

A: Call Jury Administration toll-free at    
1-800-842-8175 from anywhere within  
 
the	United	States,	or	visit	our	website		
	
at www.jud.ct.gov/jury/
 

 

 

Q: Are members of the military   
 

automatically exempt?

A: Not automatically, however, your
  military service may result in you being
excused on the basis of extreme hardship.
 

Q: Who may be excused for extreme  
 

hardship?

A: Active duty military personnel serving
 
 
 
 

out of state, on board a ship or overseas,  
or on a Connecticut military base during  
time of war may be excused on the    
basis of extreme hardship.

Q: May civilian personnel employed at a  
  Connecticut military base be excused  
 

for extreme hardship?

A: Civilian personnel must report for jury  
service, however, jury service may be  
 
postponed to a more convenient date. 
 

Q: I am not a Connecticut resident, but  
 
 
 

I am serving on a Connecticut military  
base. Am I required to serve as a juror  
in a Connecticut Superior Court?

A: No. Only legal residents of the State of
  Connecticut are eligible to serve as
 

jurors in a state court.

Q: If I am serving overseas, or on board
 
 
 

a ship, or out of state, or on a Connect- 
icut military base during time of war,  
how may I be excused from jury service?

A: If you are overseas, or on board a ship,
 
 
 

or out of state you may write a letter to  
the Jury Administrator that includes 
the following:

• Your branch of the military service
• Your rank and duty station 
• Your expected date of return to  
  Connecticut

 

 
If you are serving on a Connecticut
  military base during time of war, you  
	 may	have	your	commanding	officer		 	
  write a letter to the Jury Administrator  
 

that includes the following:

• Your branch of the military service
• Your rank and duty station
• A statement that your continued     
  presence on the base is essential to  
  the war effort

  You may forward the letter to the Jury  
	 Administrator	via	United	States	mail	at		
 

the following address:

 
 
 

 
 
 

Jury Administration
P.O. Box 260448
Hartford, CT 06126-0448

  Or, you may send an e-mail at:  
http://www.jud.ct.gov/jury/
 
Just click on “Answering your summons”.
