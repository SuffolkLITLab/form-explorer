“Nonadversarial (Simplified) Divorce”

Complete and file the following forms with the court:

•   Joint Petition - Nonadversarial Divorce (Dissolution of Marriage) (JD-FM-242)
•   Financial Affidavits (JD-FM-6) for each spouse
•   Notice of Automatic Court Orders – Nonadversarial Divorce (JD-FM-260)
•   Appearance (JD-CL-12) for each spouse
•   If either spouse has ever received public assistance: Certification of Notice in Family Cases (Public Assistance) (JD-FM-175)
•   If dividing property, money, or debt: Agreement – Nonadversarial Divorce (Dissolution of Marriage) (JD-FM-243)

Divorce may be granted in approximately 35 days without the need to see a judge.

If papers were delivered (“served”) to the other spouse and certain conditions are met, the court may waive the 90-day 
waiting period and grant the divorce without having to see a judge. If the conditions are not met,  a court date will be 
provided and you will be required to see a judge.

 “Divorce Without an Agreement – Part 1”

Step 1: Begin the process by completing the following forms and bring to the clerk’s office:

•   Summons Family Actions (JD-FM-3)
•   Divorce Complaint (Dissolution of Marriage) (JD-FM-159) 
•   Notice of Automatic Court Orders (JD-FM-158) 
•   If you need temporary orders: Motion for Orders Before Judgment (Pendente Lite) in Family Cases (JD-FM-176)
•   If either spouse has ever received public assistance: Certification of Notice in Family Cases (Public Assistance) (JD-FM-175)
NOTE:

Step 2: Deliver (“serve”) the completed forms, along with a blank Appearance (JD-CL-12) to the other spouse

 Your spouse can agree to waive service by completing and filing a Certification of Waiver of Service of Process – Divorce, 

Legal Separation, Annulment (JD-FM-249) and an Appearance (JD-CL-12)

Step 3: After paperwork has been delivered (“served”) to the other spouse, return the original paperwork with proof of delivery to 
the clerk’s office
“Divorce Without an Agreement – Part 2�

If other spouse files an appearance and there is still no agreement, you and your spouse must wait the required 90 days for the 
Case Management Date. A conference is scheduled this day to determine how your case will proceed. File the following forms with 
the court on or before the Case Management Date:

•   Case Management Agreement/Order (JD-FM-163)
•   Financial Affidavits (JD-FM-6)
•   Parenting Plan (if custody of minor children in dispute)

You and your spouse may be required to see a judge on the Case Management Date depending on the issues.
