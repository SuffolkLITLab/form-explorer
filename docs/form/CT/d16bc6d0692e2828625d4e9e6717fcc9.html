Instructions 
1. Only use this form to ask the court to erase convictions for offenses committed before January 1, 2000. 
2. Complete the form, swear to the contents, and sign it before a Notary Public, Commissioner of the Superior Court, 

Clerk, or other proper officer. 

3. Attach the following documents to this form when you file it: 

a. A copy of your Criminal History Record from the State Police Criminal Records Unit. 
b. If you were released on parole or special parole and did not have probation as part of your sentence, a letter 
from the Department of Corrections (DOC) confirming that you have completed parole for every offense for 
which you have ever been convicted. 

c. If you had probation as part of your sentence for any conviction, a letter from Adult Probation Services 

confirming that you have completed probation for every offense for which you have ever been convicted. 
4. File the form in the court location where you were sentenced for the crime that you are asking the court to erase. 
If you were sentenced in a court that has since closed, see the chart on the back of this form to find where you 
should file your petition.

Where to get your Criminal History Record: 
The State Police Criminal Records Unit keeps the official record of criminal convictions. The State Police Criminal Records 
Unit requires all requests for a copies of Criminal History Records to be made using the State of Connecticut Criminal History 
Record Request Form (DPS-0846-C) and mailed to: 

Department of Emergency Services and Public Protection 
State Police Bureau of Identification 
1111 Country Club Road 
Middletown, CT 06457 
(860) 658-8480 

Note: General Statutes § 29-11(c)(iii) requires the State Police Criminal Records Unit to charge $75 for Criminal History 
Record requests.
